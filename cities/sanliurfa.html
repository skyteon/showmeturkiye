Sanliurfa

The city of the prophets, home to the oldest temple on Earth

Places to Visit

01. Gobekli Tepe
Gobekli Tepe

The oldest known monumental temple complex on Earth, built 11,000 years ago by hunter-gatherers. T-shaped limestone pillars carved with animal reliefs, arranged in circles. UNESCO World Heritage, protected under a modern canopy. Twenty minutes outside Urfa.

02. Balikligol
Pool of Sacred Carp

Tradition places Abraham's miraculous survival of the pyre here, with the fish protecting him. Two peaceful pools filled with sacred carp, surrounded by 16th-century Ottoman mosques, arcades and gardens. A deeply atmospheric place at any time of day.

03. Urfa Old Bazaar
Urfa Old Bazaar

One of the most atmospheric bazaars in Turkiye. Covered hans, kebabs sizzling over charcoal, copper, textiles, spices and the famous Gumruk Hani coffee courtyard. Early morning is best before the midday heat.

04. Urfa Castle
Urfa Castle

The ancient citadel on the hill above Balikligol, with the two famous Corinthian columns known as the Throne of Nimrod. Walk up for sunset views over the old city and the Mevlid-i Halil mosque. Cool breeze in the evenings.

05. Harran
Harran

Fifty kilometres south of Urfa, near the Syrian border. The ruins of the ancient astronomy university of Harran, the beehive mud-brick village houses still inhabited, and the ruins of one of the oldest mosques in Anatolia. Half a day minimum.

06. Mevlid-i Halil Mosque
Mevlid-i Halil and Abraham's Cave

The cave where Muslim tradition holds that prophet Abraham was born and hidden for the first ten years of his life. Now a pilgrimage site with separate entrances for men and women. Modest dress required, very atmospheric.

07. Taş Tepeler
Taş Tepeler (Stone Hills)

The broader cultural landscape of prehistoric sites surrounding Şanlıurfa, of which Göbekli Tepe is the most famous. Multiple Neolithic hilltop sites — Karahantepe, Taş Tepeler, Gürcütepe — have been excavated since 2019, revealing a civilisation of monumental stone architecture that predates agriculture. A UNESCO designation covering the wider landscape is being pursued. Guided tours from Şanlıurfa can visit multiple sites in a single day.

08. Soğmatar Antik Kenti
Sogmatar Ancient Site

A Syriac Christian and Pagan cult site in the Tek Tek Mountains east of Şanlıurfa, with a cave temple, rock-cut Syriac inscriptions and a hilltop sanctuary to the moon god Sin. One of the most atmospheric and least visited sites in southeastern Turkey. The drive through the Tek Tek limestone plateau — a lunar landscape with scattered stone settlements — is itself a reason to come.

Food and Cuisine

Urfa food is southeastern Anatolia at its boldest. Charcoal grills, hand-chopped raw meat, slow-roasted lamb, deep red spices and the mysterious smoky Urfa pepper flakes. Meals are communal, long and loud. Few cities in Turkiye take food more seriously than Urfa.

The Signature
Urfa Kebab

Skewers of hand-chopped lamb seasoned with salt and Urfa pepper, grilled over charcoal until the fat renders. Served on flatbread with grilled tomato, pepper and a pile of raw onion. Simple, and the benchmark for Turkish grilled meat.

Raw Meat Ritual
Cig Kofte

Raw minced lamb pounded with bulgur, Urfa pepper, onion, parsley and a dozen spices, kneaded by hand for hours. Wrapped in lettuce with lemon. Traditional versions at a specialist like Haci Arif are unforgettable.

Breakfast Feast
Tirit and Halep Tava

Slow-cooked lamb and chickpeas ladled over day-old flatbread soaked in the broth. A breakfast dish that sustains you for hours. Served with pomegranate syrup and raw onion. Haci Mahmut has been making it for three generations.

Sweet Finish
Kunefe

Shredded pastry layered with unsalted cheese, baked until golden, drenched in syrup. Served hot with clotted cream on top. Hatay and Urfa both claim the best version. Try for yourself, you cannot go wrong here.

Musical Evening
Sira Gecesi

Urfa's traditional gathering, a long evening of live music, raki, mezes and storytelling in a restored stone house. Cevahir Konuk Evi runs these regularly. One of the most authentic cultural experiences in Turkiye.

Morning Staple
Urfa Breakfast

Olives, white cheese, honey, butter, soft flatbread, grilled liver, eggs with sucuk, tomato and cucumber, and plenty of strong tea. Served in the open courtyards of old Urfa houses. An unhurried two-hour affair.

Where to Eat

Top Restaurants in Trabzon

4.5 stars and above, 200 or more reviews on Google

Cevahir Konuk Evi
★★★★★ 4.6 (2,300+ reviews)

A beautifully restored 200-year-old stone mansion serving traditional Urfa cuisine in stone courtyards. Known for hosting proper sira gecesi evenings. The full Urfa experience in one place.

Traditional Urfa, Sira gecesi
Kekliklerde
★★★★★ 4.6 (3,100+ reviews)

The most famous Urfa kebab restaurant, open for three generations. Charcoal grills turning constantly. Start with cig kofte, move to Urfa kebab and finish with kunefe. A pilgrimage for anyone serious about kebabs.

Urfa kebab
Gulhan Restaurant
★★★★★ 4.5 (1,900+ reviews)

A family-run classic serving slow-cooked lamb dishes and Urfa's regional specialities. The stuffed intestine and mumbar sarma are famous. A proper lunch place with a local crowd and reasonable prices.

Home-style Urfa
Haci Arif Cig Kofte
★★★★★ 4.7 (1,800+ reviews)

A specialist in traditional hand-pounded raw meat cig kofte. A small unassuming shop in the old town where the kofte masters work in view. Wrap in lettuce with lemon and eat standing. A classic Urfa snack.

Cig kofte
Gumruk Hani
★★★★★ 4.5 (2,700+ reviews)

A 16th-century Ottoman han in the heart of the bazaar, now a shaded courtyard cafe. Menengic coffee, mirra (strong bitter coffee) and backgammon under old stone arches. The most atmospheric coffee break in the city.

Courtyard cafe
Harran Evi
★★★★★ 4.4 (1,100+ reviews)

A restored house in Harran village with seating on traditional low cushions. Local stews, bulgur pilav and mutton dishes. A pleasant lunch stop on the Harran day trip.

Rural Harran food
Haci Mahmut
★★★★★ 4.6 (1,400+ reviews)

The pilgrimage spot for tirit, the Urfa breakfast of slow-cooked lamb on soaked flatbread. Lunch only, open early, closes by mid-afternoon. A father-son operation on the same corner for 50 years.

Tirit
Urfa Konukevi
★★★★★ 4.5 (1,600+ reviews)

A restored city mansion that also serves as a small boutique hotel. Dinners in the stone courtyard feature slow-cooked regional dishes. Particularly special on an evening with live music.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Gobekli Tepe Half Day

Go early morning to beat the heat. The modern visitor centre, the walkways around the T-pillars and the new Karahan Tepe site nearby. Hire a local guide for the best context, especially about the pre-agricultural origins of the temple.

02
Harran and Desert

A full day south of Urfa to Harran. The beehive mud houses, the ancient university ruins and, if the security situation allows, a view to the Syrian border. Lunch at Harran Evi. Dry, windy, and unforgettable.

03
Balikligol Evening

Go at sunset when the heat breaks and the gardens around the sacred pools fill with local families. Feed the carp, visit the mosques, then climb up to Urfa castle as the call to prayer echoes from a dozen minarets below. The moment that Urfa is all about.

04
Old City Walking

Half a day wandering the covered bazaar, stopping at Gumruk Hani for coffee, weaving through the stone-house neighbourhoods of Kara Meydan. Finish with a visit to the Urfa Archaeology Museum, where many Gobekli Tepe finds are displayed.

05
Sira Gecesi Night

A traditional Urfa evening with live saz, traditional dances, a long mezze and raki menu, and local storytelling. Cevahir Konuk Evi runs the most authentic versions. Go as a group if you can, more singing and more fun.

06
Karahan Tepe

The sister site to Gobekli Tepe, discovered more recently and still being excavated. About 40 kilometres from Gobekli Tepe, less crowded, with slightly different carvings. Often combined with Gobekli Tepe on the same day tour.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Trabzon

185 km East, about 2.5 hours
Mardin

The golden stone city on a hillside overlooking the Mesopotamian plain. Assyrian monasteries, narrow stone lanes, panoramic rooftop restaurants and some of the most beautiful courtyards in Turkiye. At least one overnight is essential.

150 km West, about 2 hours
Halfeti

The drowned village on the Euphrates, half-submerged after the Birecik Dam. Boat trips over stone houses and a minaret rising from the water. Also known for the rare black rose that grows only here. A half-day trip with lunch by the water.
