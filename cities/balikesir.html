Balikesir

Overview

Places to Visit in Balikesir

A

Ayvalık

This charming town is located on the Aegean coast and is known for its beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and delicious seafood.

B

Cunda Island

Cunda Island, also known as Alibey Island, is a small island in the Aegean Sea off the coast of Turkey. It has rich history with evidence of human settlement dating back to ancient times. Once an important center for olive oil production and trade, the island's old stone houses and narrow streets reflect traditional architecture. Today it's a popular tourist destination known for scenic beauty, crystal-clear waters, and charming villages. Visitors enjoy swimming, sunbathing, water sports, and exploring historic sites. The island is famous for local cuisine featuring fresh seafood and traditional dishes made with olive oil.

C

Taksiyarhis Church

Historic Greek Orthodox church in Ayvalık built in the 19th century during Ottoman era, named after patron saint of sailors, Saint Nicholas. Known for impressive architecture combining Byzantine and Ottoman styles with intricate stone carvings, large dome, and interior frescoes depicting Bible scenes. After the 1923 population exchange, it was used as a mosque before being abandoned. Restored in the 1990s and converted into a cultural center hosting exhibitions and concerts.

D

Sarımsaklı Beach

Popular tourist destination in Ayvalık known for crystal-clear waters, fine white sand, and stunning scenery. Beach stretches over 7 kilometers surrounded by pine forests and olive groves. Shallow calm water ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and windsurfing. Facilities include sun loungers, umbrellas, and beach cafes. Popular with families offering water slides and playgrounds.

E

Badavut Beach

Beautiful secluded beach on Aegean coast near Ayvalık known for pristine natural beauty, clear waters, and peaceful atmosphere. Surrounded by pine forests and olive groves with long stretch of white sand. Less developed giving it more secluded and tranquil feel. Located about 10 kilometers from Ayvalık.

F

Şeytan Sofrası

Rock formation on mountain top in Kaz Mountains National Park, about 40km south of city center. Legend says the devil used to sit and dine on this rock formation. Popular spot for hiking, camping, and enjoying stunning views of surrounding mountains and forests. Hike takes 1-2 hours and is moderately difficult.

G

Kaz Mountains National Park

Must-visit destination for nature lovers. Home to diverse range of flora and fauna including oak and chestnut trees, bears, and wolves.

H

Kyzikos Ancient City

Ancient city in southeastern Marmara Sea, once prosperous and important commerce center. Explore ruins including impressive city walls, Acropolis with Temple of Hadrian, theater and agora, Roman Bridge, and Archaeological Museum featuring artifacts from the ancient city.

I

Hasanboguldu Canyon

Canyon located in Kaz Mountains, popular spot for hiking and camping. Home to beautiful waterfalls and stunning views.

J

Kapıdağ Peninsula

Beautiful rugged peninsula in southern Balıkesir, popular for adventure, hiking, and natural beauty. Rich history with sites including ancient city of Dascylium and Ayvalık Islands.

K

Edremit Gulf

Beautiful stretch of coastline in western Balıkesir. Popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Why Visit Balikesir?

Balıkesir connects the Aegean Sea to the Sea of Marmara, packing beaches, thermal springs, and mountain villages into one province. Ayvalık and Cunda Island give you Greek architecture and seafood restaurants where octopus hangs drying in the sun. Head inland and you hit Kaz Mountains, where locals swear the air itself tastes different. The region moves slow enough to actually relax but stays interesting enough to keep exploring, and you can swim in water that shifts between emerald and sapphire depending on the depth.

Food & Cuisine

Keskek

Traditional dish made from wheat and meat (usually lamb or beef) cooked in large pot for hours until it becomes porridge-like consistency. Usually served with melted butter and chili flakes.

İncir Uyutması

Dessert made from dried figs soaked in water then mixed with ground walnuts and sugar. The mixture is baked in the oven and served with cream.

Kaz Helv ası

Dessert made from the breast of a goose, sugar, and flour. The breast is cooked until tender, then mixed with sugar and flour and cooked again until it becomes a sweet and sticky dessert.

Activities & Experiences

Paragliding

Kaz Mountains provide perfect setting for paragliding. Soar above the mountain range and enjoy breathtaking views of surrounding landscape.

Rock Climbing

Kaz Mountains and Kapıdağ Peninsula offer great opportunities for rock climbing. Several climbing routes available for beginners and experienced climbers.

Rafting

Balıkesir has several rivers offering great rafting opportunities. Kocaçay River is particularly popular for rafting enthusiasts.

Trekking and Hiking

Several hiking trails wind through Kaz Mountains and Kapıdağ Peninsula offering stunning views and great way to explore natural beauty.

Mountain Biking

Kapıdağ Peninsula particularly popular among mountain bikers with rugged terrain and stunning coastal views.

Near Places to Visit

Bursa: Larger bustling city about 180 kilometers away known for historical landmarks, rich cultural heritage, and modern industries. Top attractions include Green Mosque, Bursa Castle, Grand Mosque, and Bursa City Museum. Famous for traditional Turkish baths, thermal springs, and Iskender kebab.
