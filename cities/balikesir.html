Why Visit

Balıkesir

Balıkesir is the kind of province that rewards people who look past the obvious. It covers a large stretch of coast between the Marmara Sea and the northern Aegean, holds Ayvalık, one of the best-preserved Greek trading towns in Turkey, and contains the Kapıdağ Peninsula — a pine-covered finger of land projecting into the Marmara with almost no tourist infrastructure and some of the calmest bays in the region.

Ayvalık is the anchor. The town has been an olive oil city for centuries and the architecture reflects the prosperity that came with it: stone houses, church towers, factory chimneys now repurposed as boutique hotels. Cunda Island, connected by causeway, holds the restored Taksiyarhis Church and some of the best seafood meyhanes in the country. 22 small islands surround the bay, reachable by boat in an afternoon.

Further north, the Manyas Kuşcenneti National Park holds 266 bird species including breeding crested pelicans and spoonbills in an area smaller than most city parks. It sits on the main bird migration corridor between Asia, Europe and Africa and is almost entirely unknown to international tourism. The Gönen thermal springs, known since antiquity, give the inland part of the province a different reason to stop.

Places to Visit

01. Ayvalık Old Town
Ayvalık

A former Greek olive oil city evacuated in the 1923 population exchange and resettled. The old town survives largely intact: stone townhouses, a clocktower mosque (the Saatlı Cami, the converted church of Agios Yannis), narrow market lanes and the smell of olive oil from small-scale producers still operating in the old factory buildings. The waterfront fills with fishing boats in the morning and the evening light is exceptional. 22 islands are visible from the shore.

02. Cunda Island
Cunda (Alibey) Adası

Connected to Ayvalık by causeway, Cunda was a prosperous Greek island until 1923 and the stone architecture of that period survives in considerable density. The Taksiyarhis Church, a large 19th-century Greek Orthodox cathedral, has been restored and operates as a Rahmi M. Koç museum. The waterfront meyhanes are the best place to eat seafood in the province. The island is walkable and the back streets are worth an afternoon. Come for dinner and stay to watch the boats come in at dusk.

03. Erdek and Kapıdağ Peninsula
Erdek / Kapıdağ

Erdek is the gateway to the Kapıdağ Peninsula, once an island and now connected to the mainland by a narrow tombolo, projecting 30 km into the Marmara Sea. The peninsula is covered in pine and olive forest with very little development. The road circumnavigating it is one of the best coastal drives in the region. The ancient city of Kyzikos, once one of the wealthiest cities of the ancient Greek world, lies ruined on the peninsula floor. Ferries from Erdek connect to Avşa, Marmara and Paşalimanı islands.

04. Manyas Kuşcenneti
Bird Paradise National Park

Turkey's smallest national park by protected area and arguably its most ecologically significant. The park sits on the northeast shore of Lake Manyas on the main migration route between Asia, Europe and Africa. In spring and summer, crested pelicans, spoonbills, night herons, cormorants and great white pelicans nest in the waterside poplars in visible colonies. The observation tower gives direct sightlines into the nesting area. 266 bird species recorded. Open Tuesday to Sunday. 18 km south of Bandırma.

05. Balıkesir City Centre
Balıkesir Merkez

The provincial capital sits inland and is rarely on the tourist circuit. The Zağanos Paşa Camii, a 15th-century Ottoman mosque with an unusual double minaret plan, is the most significant architectural monument in the city. The covered bazaar around it dates to the same period. The archaeological museum covers finds from across the province including the ancient sites of Kyzikos and Assos. A stopover rather than a destination but worth a morning if passing through.

06. Gönen Thermal Baths
Gönen Kaplıcaları

Gönen has been known for its thermal springs since antiquity. The water reaches 78 degrees at source and feeds a series of hotels and public bath facilities in the town. Several hotels in Gönen pump the thermal water directly into private room baths. The springs are rich in radon and minerals and attract a largely domestic Turkish clientele year round. A working, unpretentious alternative to the more touristic spa resorts found elsewhere in Turkey. 60 km north of Balıkesir city centre.

07. Ayvalık Islands National Park
Ayvalık Adaları Milli Parkı

An archipelago of 22 islands off the Ayvalık coast, designated a national park, with crystal-clear Aegean water, pine forest and the famous Şeytan Sofrası (Devil's Dinner Table) viewpoint on Alibey Island. Boat trips from Ayvalık harbour visit multiple islands with swimming stops. The light on the water in the late afternoon, with the islands silhouetted against the Aegean, is exceptional.

08. Edremit Gulf and Olive Groves
Edremit Körfezi

The Edremit Gulf — the southernmost bay of the Aegean Turkish coast — is flanked by olive groves that produce some of Turkey's finest olive oil, with Kaz Dağları (Mount Ida, of Trojan War fame) rising directly behind the coastal towns of Altınoluk, Akçay and Edremit. The combination of olive culture, Aegean coast and mythological landscape makes this corner of Balıkesir worth a dedicated visit.

Local Cuisine

What to Eat

Balıkesir's food is built on olive oil. The province, particularly around Ayvalık, Edremit and Burhaniye, produces some of Turkey's finest extra virgin olive oil, and it appears in everything: the mezes, the fish preparations, the breads and the pastries. The Marmara and Aegean coastlines mean fresh seafood throughout the year. Inland, cooking shifts toward lamb and slow-cooked legumes.

The Foundation
Ayvalık Zeytinyağı

Ayvalık and the Edremit Gulf coast produce early-harvest extra virgin olive oil with low acidity and a distinctive grassy, slightly peppery finish. The Ayvalık variety olive is a protected geographical indication and the best oil comes from producers who harvest in October before full ripeness. Several Ayvalık producers sell directly from the press. A litre of properly labeled Ayvalık olive oil bought at source is one of the best value food purchases in Turkey.

Cunda Specialty
Aegean Meze

The meyhanes on Cunda waterfront serve a meze spread specific to this coastline: sea urchin roe when in season (October to March), stuffed mussels, smoked sardine, fried courgette with garlic yogurt, fava bean puree, marinated octopus. Order five or six cold mezes and share across the table before the fish arrives. The sea urchin roe alone is reason enough to time a visit to Cunda in late autumn or winter.

Daily Catch
Ayvalık Usulü Balık

Grilled sea bass, sea bream or red mullet with local olive oil and lemon. The fish restaurants along the Ayvalık and Cunda waterfront display the daily catch on ice at the entrance. Order by weight, ask what came in that morning, and eat it simply. The proximity of the olive groves to the water means the oil in the good restaurants is often local and genuinely fresh.

Breakfast Pastry
Balıkesir Poğaçası

Balıkesir is well known across Turkey for its poğaça, a yeasted, slightly flaky pastry filled with white cheese and black olives, or minced meat. The version specific to this province uses a soft, enriched dough and is larger than the standard Istanbul bakery version. Sold from street bakeries in the early morning in Balıkesir city and from pastane shops in Ayvalık's market lanes. Best eaten warm from the oven, minutes after baking.

Inland Dish
Keşkek

A slow-cooked preparation of wheat groats and lamb, beaten together for hours until the mixture reaches a smooth, porridge-like consistency. Keşkek is made for weddings and large celebrations in the inland villages of Balıkesir Province. The version from this region uses lamb from the Kazdağları foothills and local wheat. Hard to find in ordinary restaurants but occasionally available at lokanta-style canteens in the city and at larger village celebrations.

Local Institution
Ayvalık Tostu

A specific style of toasted sandwich made with dense Ayvalık bread, filled with sucuk or cheese and grilled until the bread is dark and crunchy. The Ayvalık toast has a following across Turkey and is eaten for breakfast or late at night. Several cafes in Ayvalık specialize in nothing else. The bread, slightly denser and more chewy than standard white sandwich bread, is what makes the difference. Available throughout the day from street cafes in the old town.

Where to Eat

Recommended Restaurants

Cunda Island has the best concentration of seafood restaurants in the province. Ayvalık old town has good cafes and breakfast spots in the market area.

Cunda Körfez Restaurant

On the Cunda waterfront with views across the bay to Ayvalık. The meze spread is the main reason to come: sea urchin in season, octopus, stuffed mussels and half a dozen cold Aegean preparations before the grilled fish. Book ahead at weekends in summer and aim for a terrace table at sunset.

Aegean Meze, Grilled Fish, Sea Urchin
Taş Kahve Cunda
★★★★★ 4.6 · 900+ reviews

A historic stone teahouse and restaurant overlooking the Cunda harbour in a converted Greek-era building. Good for a long breakfast or lunch in the garden terrace. The kitchen serves Aegean mezes and grilled fish. The atmosphere and view are as important as the food. Arrive before 10:00 for breakfast before the day-trip crowds arrive from the mainland.

Breakfast, Aegean Mezes, Tea Garden
Şeytan'ın Kahvesi
★★★★★ 4.5 · 700+ reviews

A small historic cafe in Ayvalık's back lanes with a Greek-era atmosphere. The breakfast menu is the draw: Ayvalık olive oil, local olives, fresh bread, eggs and cheese. The interior is cluttered with old photographs and Greek-era objects. Arrive early on weekends, particularly in summer, when every table fills by 09:00.

Ayvalık Breakfast, Local Olive Oil
Balıkesir Çarşı Lokantası
★★★★☆ 4.4 · 500+ reviews

A no-nonsense lokanta in Balıkesir city centre near the Zağanos Paşa Camii. The daily menu is written on a board: lamb stew, stuffed peppers, lentil soup and seasonal vegetables. Cheap, filling and genuinely good. Eaten at shared tables by local workers and market vendors. Closes by 15:00.

Experiences

Things to Do

01
Bird Watching at Manyas

The peak breeding season is March to July. Come before 08:00 when the nesting colonies are most active and the light is best for photography. The observation tower gives direct sightlines into the pelican and spoonbill colonies at close range. Bring binoculars and a telephoto lens if you have one; the park loans binoculars at the entrance. The drive from Bandırma takes 20 minutes. Open Tuesday to Sunday. Combined with an afternoon in Erdek, this makes a full day without rushing.

02
Kapıdağ Peninsula Circuit

The road circumnavigating the Kapıdağ Peninsula from Erdek is approximately 95 km and takes three to four hours without stops. Several small bays along the north shore have no infrastructure and are reachable by a short walk from the road. The ruins of Kyzikos, once among the richest cities of the ancient Greek world, lie in open fields near Erdek and are freely accessible. A car is required; there is no public transport on the peninsula. Best done early in the day before the heat builds in summer.

03
Ayvalık Island Boat Trip

Day-long boat tours from Ayvalık harbour visit several of the 22 islands in the archipelago, stopping at swimming bays with clear North Aegean water. Tours run June to October, departing around 10:00 and returning by 17:00. The Devil's Dinner Table rock formation viewpoint, Seytan Sofrasi, gives a panoramic view over the island chain at sunset and is reachable by car or taxi from Ayvalık. Good for photography in late afternoon when the light is behind the islands.

Beyond the Centre

Day Trips

90 km south, about 1.5 hrs
Assos and Behramkale

The ancient city of Assos, founded in the 8th century BC, sits on a volcanic promontory above the Aegean with a Temple of Athena still standing at the summit. Aristotle lived and taught here for three years. Below the acropolis, the village of Behramkale has preserved Ottoman-era stone houses and a small harbour with a fishing community. The approach road from the north, dropping through olive groves to the sea, is one of the most atmospheric drives in northwestern Turkey.

Ferry from Erdek, 1 to 2 hrs
Marmara Islands

Regular ferries from Erdek and Bandırma connect to the Marmara Islands: Avşa, Marmara and Paşalimanı. Avşa is the most popular, with sandy beaches and a small resort infrastructure. Marmara Island has the marble quarries that supplied Constantinople and several Byzantine-era structures. Paşalimanı is the quietest, with olive groves and almost no development. A one-night stay on any of them gives access to bays impossible to reach by any other means.
