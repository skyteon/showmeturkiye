Cappadocia

Overview

Places to Visit in Cappadocia

A

Cappadocia Region

Cappadocia is a historical and geological region in central Turkey famous for unique rock formations, underground cities, and ancient cave dwellings. Best time to visit is spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) when weather is mild. Popular activities include hot air balloon rides at sunrise over stunning rock formations, visiting underground cities used by early Christians, exploring the Göreme Open Air Museum (UNESCO World Heritage Site), hiking through valleys and rock formations, and experiencing local villages, markets, and cuisine. Cave hotels offer unique accommodation experiences.

B

Kaymaklı Underground City

This underground city is one of the largest in Cappadocia and was used by early Christians as a hiding place from persecution. Visitors can explore the tunnels, rooms, and passages that make up the city.

C

Göreme National Park

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a must-visit destination in Nevşehir. The park is famous for its unique rock formations and cave dwellings, which were once used as homes and places of worship by early Christians.

D

Uchisar Castle

This castle is situated on a hill and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. It was once used as a watchtower and was later turned into a fortress.

E

Paşabağ (Fairy Chimneys)

This is another unique rock formation in Cappadocia. The area is known for its fairy chimneys, which are tall, cone-shaped rock formations that were created by volcanic activity.

F

Devrent Valley (Imagination Valley)

This valley is also known as "Imagination Valley" due to its unusual rock formations that resemble animals and other objects. Visitors can take a walk through the valley and try to spot these formations.

G

Avanos

This town is located on the banks of the Kızılırmak River and is known for its pottery-making tradition. Visitors can watch artisans at work and purchase pottery items as souvenirs.

H

Derinkuyu Underground City

This is another underground city in Cappadocia and is believed to be the deepest one in the region. It has several levels and was used as a hiding place and shelter by early Christians.

I

Zelve Open Air Museum

This museum is located in a valley and is home to several rock-cut churches and dwellings. Visitors can explore the museum and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Why Visit Cappadocia?

Cappadocia carved itself into volcanic rock over millions of years, and humans have been adding to the landscape ever since. You can wake up to hot air balloons drifting past your cave hotel window, explore underground cities that housed thousands of people, and hike through valleys where churches hide frescoes painted a thousand years ago. The terrain looks otherworldly but the experience stays grounded: family-run restaurants in Göreme, pottery workshops in Avanos, and sunrise views that work without any Instagram filter.

Food & Cuisine

Testi Kebabı

This is a traditional Turkish dish that is popular in Nevşehir. It is made with lamb or beef that is slow-cooked with vegetables and spices in a clay pot. The pot is sealed and cooked in a wood-fired oven, giving the dish a unique flavor and aroma.

Mantı

This is a Turkish version of dumplings that are filled with ground meat and spices. They are served with a tomato and garlic sauce and topped with yogurt.

Nevşehir Baklavas ı

This is a local variation of the popular Turkish pastry, baklava. It is made with a thin, crispy pastry and filled with pistachios and syrup.

Activities & Experiences

Hot Air Balloon

Hot air balloon is a popular activity in Cappadocia and offers a unique way to experience the stunning landscape. Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the fairy chimneys, valleys, and rock formations of the region at sunrise.

ATV Tours

ATV tours are a popular way to explore the rugged terrain of Cappadocia. Visitors can rent ATVs and take guided tours through the valleys and hills, offering a thrilling adventure and a chance to see the landscape from a different perspective.

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding tours are another popular way to explore the Cappadocia region. Visitors can take guided tours on horseback through the valleys and rock formations, providing a unique way to experience the landscape.

Paragliding

Paragliding is a thrilling activity that allows visitors to fly over the stunning landscape of Cappadocia. Visitors can take tandem paragliding flights with experienced pilots, offering a bird's eye view of the fairy chimneys and valleys.

Rock Climbing

The unique rock formations and valleys of Cappadocia offer several opportunities for rock climbing. Visitors can take guided tours with experienced climbers and explore the various climbing routes throughout the region.

Near Places to Visit

Kayseri: Kayseri is the largest city in the region, located about 80 kilometers east of Nevşehir. Known for its historical landmarks including Kayseri Castle and the impressive Mount Erciyes that overlooks the city.

Aksaray: Aksaray is a city located about 60 kilometers southwest of Nevşehir. Known for its historical landmarks including the Ulu Mosque and the Aksaray Museum.
