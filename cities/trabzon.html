Trabzon

Overview

Places to Visit in Trabzon

A

Sümela Monastery

A stunning Greek Orthodox monastery built into a cliff face in the Altındere Valley. Dating back to the 4th century, it offers breathtaking views and rich history.

B

Uzungöl

A picturesque lake surrounded by lush green mountains and traditional wooden houses. Perfect for nature lovers and photographers seeking tranquility.

C

Atatürk Pavilion

A beautiful early 20th-century mansion set in a peaceful park, showcasing elegant architecture and serving as a museum dedicated to Atatürk's visits to Trabzon.

D

Trabzon Castle

Ancient fortress walls offering panoramic views of the city and Black Sea. A perfect spot to explore Byzantine history and enjoy stunning sunset views.

E

Hagia Sophia of Trabzon

A magnificent 13th-century Byzantine church featuring stunning frescoes and mosaics. Now a museum showcasing the region's rich cultural heritage.

F

Çal Cave

The second-longest cave in Turkey, featuring impressive stalactites and stalagmites. An underground adventure through natural geological wonders.

Why Visit Trabzon?

Trabzon gives you Turkey's Black Sea coast at its most striking: monasteries built into vertical cliffs, lakes that sit perfectly still between pine-covered mountains, and a food culture that runs deep. The city moves at its own rhythm, where locals still gather in corner tea houses and fishing boats bring in fresh anchovies every morning. You'll find real mountain air here, Byzantine history that hasn't been turned into a theme park, and enough forest trails to lose yourself for days.

Food & Cuisine

Akçaabat Köfte: Trabzon's most famous dish - spiced meatballs grilled to perfection. A must-try for any visitor.

Hamsi (Anchovy): The Black Sea's staple fish, prepared in dozens of ways - fried, baked in rice, or even in desserts. Available fresh from November to April.

Kuymak: A rich, creamy dish made from cornmeal, butter, and local cheese. Perfect comfort food for cold mountain evenings.

Laz Böreği: A sweet pastry dessert filled with custard cream and dusted with powdered sugar. Originated in the nearby Laz region.

Muhlama: Similar to fondue, this melted cheese dish is mixed with cornmeal and butter, creating an incredibly indulgent experience.

Activities & Experiences

Hiking & Nature

Trabzon offers incredible hiking opportunities in the Kaçkar Mountains, Altındere National Park, and around Uzungöl. Trails range from easy walks to challenging multi-day treks.

Water Sports

Try rafting in the Fırtına Valley, kayaking in Uzungöl, or simply swimming along Trabzon's Black Sea coastline during summer months.

Cultural Tours

Explore Byzantine churches, Ottoman mosques, and traditional Black Sea villages. Join guided tours to learn about the region's rich multicultural history.

Photography

From misty mountain peaks to ancient monasteries and vibrant tea plantations, Trabzon is a photographer's paradise. Early morning and sunset offer the most dramatic light.

Near Places to Visit

Ayder Plateau: Located about 2 hours from Trabzon, Ayder is a stunning highland plateau famous for its thermal springs, traditional wooden houses, and dramatic mountain scenery. It's a perfect escape for nature lovers.

Rize: Just 45 minutes east of Trabzon, Rize is known as Turkey's tea capital. Visit sprawling tea plantations, enjoy authentic local cuisine, and explore the scenic Fırtına Valley.

Zigana Pass: A breathtaking mountain pass offering spectacular views between Trabzon and Gümüşhane. Perfect for a scenic drive and photography.
