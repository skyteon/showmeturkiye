Why Visit

Trabzon

Trabzon is one of those cities that surprises people who visit for the first time. It sits right on the Black Sea coast, backed by thick green mountains, and it has been welcoming travelers for thousands of years. From Byzantine monasteries perched on cliff faces to Ottoman mosques, from highland lakes to busy fish markets, there is always something worth seeing.

The city moves at its own pace. In the morning, fishermen bring in fresh anchovies while tea gardens fill up with locals. The old bazaar, the waterfront and the mountain roads heading south all have their own character. Trabzon is not trying to impress you. It just is what it is, and that is exactly why people love it.

Whether you have two days or a full week, Trabzon offers a proper Black Sea experience: excellent food, real history and some of the most dramatic natural landscapes in all of Turkiye.

Places to Visit

01. Sumela Monastery
Sumela Monastery

One of the most impressive sights in Turkiye. This Greek Orthodox monastery was built into a vertical cliff face in the Altindere Valley back in the 4th century. The frescoes inside have survived centuries of history. Go early in the morning for the best light and fewer crowds.

02. Uzungol
Uzungol

About 99 km from the city centre, Uzungol is a beautiful mountain lake surrounded by green hills and wooden houses. It is especially popular in autumn when the forests turn orange and gold. There are restaurants and guesthouses right on the lakeside.

03. Ataturk Pavilion
Ataturk Pavilion

Built in 1890, this elegant mansion sits in a quiet park above the city with views over the Black Sea. It was used by Ataturk during his visits to Trabzon and is now preserved as a museum. The rooms are kept exactly as they were.

04. Trabzon Castle
Trabzon Castle

The castle walls date back to the Byzantine era and the upper section is now a public park with excellent views over the harbour and the Black Sea. It is a short walk from the city centre and free to enter.

05. Hagia Sophia of Trabzon
Hagia Sophia of Trabzon

Originally built as a Byzantine church in the 13th century, this beautiful stone building holds remarkable frescoes and stonework. It is located close to the sea and easy to reach on foot from the city centre.

06. Boztepe Hill
Boztepe Hill

A hill on the eastern side of Trabzon city with panoramic views over the Black Sea, the harbour and the city. Reached by car or city bus in 15 minutes from the centre. The terrace cafés at the top serve tea with the view of the coastline that defined Trabzon's position as the Pontic capital. Best at sunset when the city lights begin to come on against the dark sea.

07. Rize-Trabzon Coast Road
Rize-Trabzon Coast Road

The coastal road along the eastern Black Sea between Trabzon and Rize is one of the most dramatic drives in Turkey — narrow highway carved into cliff faces above the water, with tea plantations on every available slope and fishing villages at the harbour mouths. The landscape changes every 10 km. Best driven west-to-east (Trabzon to Rize) in the morning light. The mid-route stop at Ardeşen for Black Sea breakfast is recommended.

08. Maçka District
Maçka District

The district south of Trabzon through which the Sumela road passes — a valley of forested mountains, small villages, stone bridges and a character distinct from the coast. The Altındere National Park, Sümela Monastery, and the hiking routes toward the Kaçkar approach are all within Maçka. The district town itself has good lokanta food and serves as the practical base for those wanting more than a day trip to Sümela.

Food and Cuisine

Trabzon has one of the most distinctive food cultures in Turkiye. The Black Sea region has its own ingredients and recipes that you will not find elsewhere. From the famous Akcaabat kofte to a proper Black Sea breakfast with kuymak and local cheese, eating well here is easy and affordable.

The Signature
Akcaabat Kofte

Trabzon's most celebrated dish. Hand-formed meatballs made from local beef, grilled over charcoal with a simple seasoning. The recipe has barely changed in generations. The dedicated kofte restaurants in the Akcaabat district, just west of the city, are considered the best.

The Coast
Hamsi

The anchovy is the fish of the Black Sea coast and locals are very proud of it. Available fresh from November to April, it is cooked pan-fried, baked into rice, folded into bread and even used in desserts. Visiting in season and ordering hamsi is not optional.

Mountain Comfort
Kuymak

A rich dish made from cornmeal, butter and local Trabzon cheese melted together in a copper pan. It is thick, cheesy and very filling. Perfect for a cold morning in the mountains. Most breakfast spots in Trabzon serve it fresh from the pan.

Sweet Finish
Laz Boregi

A beloved pastry from the eastern Black Sea region. Thin layers of dough filled with a light custard cream, baked until golden and finished with powdered sugar. Every good pastry shop in Trabzon will have a version worth trying.

The Ritual
Rize Tea

The tea gardens around Rize, just east of Trabzon, produce some of the best tea in Turkiye. Black Sea tea is strong and full-bodied, served in a small tulip glass. Locals drink it all day long. Sitting in a tea garden overlooking the sea is one of the simple pleasures here.

Morning Staple
Black Sea Breakfast

A proper Black Sea breakfast is one of the highlights of visiting Trabzon. Local cheeses, olives, honey, eggs, fresh bread, butter and a hot pan of muhlama all arrive at once. It is generous, unhurried and delicious. Many local cafes serve it on weekends.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Visit Sumela Monastery

The 45 km drive south from Trabzon into the Altindere National Park brings you to one of Turkiye's most breathtaking sites. The monastery is a 20-minute walk from the car park through a forest path. Entry tickets are purchased at the gate and the site is open daily except Mondays.

02
Boztepe Cable Car and Hill

Boztepe hill sits just above the city and offers one of the best panoramic views of Trabzon and the Black Sea. You can take the cable car from the station near the port or drive up. Several tea gardens and small restaurants at the top make it a relaxing half-day visit.

03
Uzungol Lake Day Trip

The 99 km drive from Trabzon to Uzungol passes through mountain villages and lush forest. The lake is at 1,090 metres elevation and surrounded by wooden guesthouses and restaurants. Hiking trails around the lake range from easy walks to longer routes into the surrounding hills.

04
Trabzon Old City Walk

The compact old city is easy to explore on foot in half a day. The route covers the Hagia Sophia mosque and museum, the Byzantine castle walls, the covered bedesten bazaar and the waterfront fish market. Start early to catch the morning activity at the port.

05
Kackar Mountains Trekking

The Kackar range, accessible from Trabzon in about two hours, offers trails for all levels. The Altindere Valley trail is the most popular starting point. For serious hikers, multi-day routes cross high passes above 3,000 metres during July and August when snow cover clears.

06
Coastal Drive East toward Rize

The D010 coastal road heading east from Trabzon toward Rize is a beautiful and easy drive. Tea gardens line the hillsides, the Black Sea runs alongside and you can stop at roadside tea houses along the way. The drive to Rize city centre takes about an hour and a half.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Trabzon

75 km East, about 1.5 hours
Rize and Ayder Plateau

Ayder is a highland village at 1,350 metres in the Kackar Mountains. It has natural thermal springs, mountain guesthouses and walking trails. The road up passes through the Firtina Valley with old stone Ottoman bridges. A very doable full day from Trabzon.

120 km South, about 2 hours
Zigana Pass and Gumushane

The road south from Trabzon climbs to Zigana Pass at over 2,000 metres through forests and mountain meadows. In summer the views are outstanding. Continuing south brings you to Gumushane, a historic town near the impressive Karaca Cave which is open to visitors.
