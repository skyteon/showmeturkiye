Why Visit

Canakkale

Canakkale is a city that sits at the narrowest point of the Dardanelles, the strait connecting the Aegean to the Sea of Marmara. Ferries have crossed here for thousands of years and the strategic importance of this passage shaped much of Western history. From both banks you can see the opposite shore. On a clear day the crossing by ferry takes about 15 minutes and the view of the castles on each side is one of the more quietly dramatic sights in Turkiye.

Thirty kilometres to the south lies ancient Troy. It has been inhabited continuously for 4,000 years, destroyed and rebuilt nine times, and was added to the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1998. The ruins are not the most visually spectacular in Turkey, but understanding that this specific hill has been settled since 3,600 BC and that the Trojan War legends are tied to this ground gives the visit a different weight. The on-site museum built in 2018 provides the essential context.

Across the strait, the Gallipoli Peninsula holds the battlefields of the 1915 Dardanelles Campaign. The cemeteries, memorials and landscape of Gallipoli attract visitors from Australia, New Zealand, the United Kingdom and Turkey year-round. The events of 1915 remain deeply present in the Turkish national consciousness and the battlefield interpretation reflects both sides with unusual even-handedness.

Places to Visit

01. Troy (Truva) UNESCO Site
Troy UNESCO Site

Ancient Troy sits on a hill 30 km south of Canakkale city centre, inhabited continuously from around 3,600 BC to 500 AD. Nine distinct city layers have been excavated here. The 2018 museum on site is genuinely excellent and provides the archaeological context that the ruins alone cannot supply. Go in the morning and allow three hours minimum. The Trojan Horse replica at the entrance is a crowd-pleaser, but the walls of Troy VI from around 1300 BC are the real thing.

02. Gallipoli and ANZAC Cove
Gallipoli Battlefields

ANZAC Cove is a 600-metre beach on the Gallipoli Peninsula where Australian and New Zealand troops landed on 25 April 1915. The landscape of the campaign is well preserved within a national park. Lone Pine Cemetery, Chunuk Bair, and the Kabatepe Museum all reward careful visits. A full day is the minimum to see the main sites. A guided tour from Canakkale is strongly recommended for the historical context, particularly for ANZAC Day on 25 April.

03. Kilitbahir Castle
Kilitbahir Castle

Built by Sultan Mehmed II in 1463 to control the narrowest point of the Dardanelles, this Ottoman fortress on the European shore has an unusual clover-leaf plan and a seven-storey triangular inner tower. Its name means "lock of the sea." The ferry from Canakkale takes 15 minutes and costs almost nothing. The castle and the small village around it are worth two hours. The view back across the strait to Canakkale from the castle walls is excellent.

04. Canakkale Waterfront
Canakkale Waterfront and Cimenlik Castle

The waterfront promenade runs along the Asian shore of the Dardanelles and is the social centre of the city. Cimenlik Castle, also built by Mehmed II in 1462 as the pair to Kilitbahir, sits at the southern end and now houses a naval museum with a replica of the Nusret minelayer from 1915. The Trojan Horse used in the 2004 film Troy with Brad Pitt can be seen on the waterfront. Walk the promenade in the late afternoon when the light falls on the water.

05. Assos (Behramkale)
Assos Ancient City

About 85 km south of Canakkale, the ancient city of Assos sits on a volcanic plug above the Aegean. Aristotle taught philosophy here for three years before founding his school in Athens. The Temple of Athena, built in the 6th century BC, stands at the summit with views across the water to the Greek island of Lesbos. The village of Behramkale below has stone houses, a small harbour and several good restaurants. Best visited as a full-day trip from Canakkale.

06. Alexandria Troas
Alexandria Troas Ruins

The ruins of the Hellenistic and Roman city of Alexandria Troas lie along the Aegean coast south of Canakkale. Founded in the 3rd century BC by Antigonus Monophthalmos, it became one of the most important ports in the Roman Empire. St Paul passed through twice, recording it in the Acts of the Apostles. The site is largely unexcavated and scattered through agricultural land, but the scale of the walls and baths gives a sense of how large this city once was. Rarely visited and genuinely atmospheric.

Food and Cuisine

Canakkale has a food culture built around the Dardanelles and the Aegean coast. The strait produces fish in variety and quality. Ezine cheese, made from sheep and goat milk in the Ezine district, has PDO status and is one of the most distinctive cheeses in the country. The breakfast culture here is good, the mezes are honest, and the fish restaurants along the waterfront set a reliable standard.

The Strait
Dardanelles Fish

The Dardanelles current running between the Aegean and the Sea of Marmara creates excellent conditions for sea bass, sea bream, bluefish and various smaller species. The fish restaurants along the Canakkale waterfront get daily deliveries. Ask what came in that morning and order that. Grilled simply with olive oil and lemon is the standard preparation and is consistently good. The smaller family-run places near the ferry pier are more reliable than the tourist-facing terrace spots.

PDO Cheese
Ezine Cheese

Protected Designation of Origin cheese from the Ezine district of Canakkale province, made from sheep, goat and cow milk in varying proportions. The Ezine white cheese is creamier and less salty than standard Turkish beyaz peynir, with a distinctive flavour from the local pastures on the slopes of Kaz Mountain. It is sold throughout the province and is the correct choice for the local breakfast table. Buy a block at the bazaar rather than the vacuum-packed version.

The Classic
Lamb Kebab

The Biga and Ezine districts of Canakkale province produce good lamb on open pasture and the kebab tradition reflects this. The local lamb kebab is leaner and more flavourful than feedlot alternatives. It is cooked over charcoal with minimal seasoning. Most good lokanta in the city centre serve it at lunch as part of a daily menu. Order with a bowl of yogurt and fresh bread.

Morning Table
Canakkale Breakfast

A proper Canakkale breakfast centres on Ezine cheese, local olives from the Aegean olive groves, fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, eggs and honey from the Kaz Mountain foothills. It is generous and unhurried. Served with tea in the standard tulip glass. Many guesthouses in the city prepare it with genuinely local produce, which makes it noticeably better than the generic Turkish hotel breakfast found elsewhere.

Aegean Mezes
Artichoke Mezes

Canakkale province produces significant quantities of artichokes and they appear in several local meze dishes. Artichoke cooked in olive oil with dill and lemon is a classic Aegean preparation that is particularly good here because the artichokes are local. The meze selection at good fish restaurants in Canakkale is broad, olive-oil-based and worth ordering in quantity rather than rushing to the main course.

Local Craft
Canakkale Ceramics

Not a food, but inseparable from the food culture. Canakkale has produced glazed ceramics for centuries and the city's name itself comes from the word for pottery. Local tea is served in Canakkale-made cups at many traditional tea houses and the craft market near the clock tower sells genuine local pieces alongside tourist reproductions. The originals are identifiable by their irregular glaze and the weight of the clay.

A well-regarded lokanta near the city centre serving Turkish home cooking. The daily rotating menu covers stews, grilled meats and Aegean dishes. Very popular with locals at lunch. No reservations, but it is large enough to accommodate most walk-ins outside peak hours.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Morning at Troy

The site opens at 8am and the first hour before tour buses arrive is the best time to be there. The museum opens slightly later but is worth waiting for. Allow three hours for both the museum and the ruins. The Trojan Horse replica is at the entrance. The walls of Troy VI, which date to around 1300 BC and are the layer most associated with the legendary war, are the most impressive structural remains on site.

02
Guided Gallipoli Tour

The Gallipoli battlefields require a full day and a guide who knows the ground. Several reputable operators run small-group tours from Canakkale that cover ANZAC Cove, Lone Pine, Chunuk Bair and the Turkish memorials. The personal testimonies embedded in the landscape, including the famous order from Mustafa Kemal to his soldiers in 1915, require an interpreter who can explain both the military history and its ongoing significance to Turks and Anzacs alike.

03
Ferry to Kilitbahir

The public ferry from Canakkale to Kilitbahir runs regularly throughout the day and the crossing takes about 15 minutes. The castle and the small village on the European shore are worth a morning. Walking the castle walls gives a clear picture of why Mehmed II built here and the view across the strait to Canakkale is one of the better ones in the region. Combine it with a walk through the village and lunch at one of the small fish restaurants by the water.

Further Afield

Day Trips

85 km South, about 1 hour 30 min
Assos and Behramkale

The ancient city of Assos perches on a volcanic summit above the Aegean with Aristotle's connection and a 6th-century BC Temple of Athena at the top. The village of Behramkale below has a stone Ottoman bridge, traditional houses, a small harbour and good fish restaurants. The view from the temple across to Lesbos on a clear day is exceptional. Plan a full day and descend to the harbour for dinner before the return drive.

ferry from Geyikli, about 2 hours from Canakkale
Bozcaada Island

Turkey's third largest island sits in the northern Aegean and is known for its vineyards, Venetian and Ottoman castle, clean beaches and very quiet streets. The island produces its own wine from indigenous grape varieties and the winery visits are accessible. The ferry from Geyikli takes about 35 minutes. In July and August the island is busy. In May, June and September it is considerably more pleasant and considerably less crowded.
