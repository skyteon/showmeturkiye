Places to Visit

01. Mevlana Museum
Mevlana Museum

The tomb of the 13th-century mystic Rumi, set under a turquoise-tiled dome that is the symbol of Konya. The complex includes the dervish lodge, ceremonial hall, kitchens and dormitories. Free entry, modest dress, head covering for women.

02. Alaeddin Mosque
Alaeddin Mosque and Hill

A 12th-century Seljuk mosque on the artificial hill at the centre of the old city. Wooden columns reused from earlier buildings, a tomb of eight Seljuk sultans next door. The hilltop park is a beloved local hangout in the evenings.

03. Karatay Medrese
Karatay Medrese (Tile Museum)

A 1251 Seljuk theological school with a star-shaped dome covered in turquoise and indigo tiles. Now a museum of Anatolian ceramic art, with the most beautiful Seljuk tile collection in the country. Compact, jewel-like.

04. Ince Minare Medrese
Ince Minare Medrese

Another Seljuk school, famous for its intricate stone-carved portal. The minaret was struck by lightning and shortened. Now a museum of stone and wood carving with reliefs from across Anatolia.

05. Catalhoyuk
Catalhoyuk

The 9,000-year-old Neolithic settlement, the world\u0027s oldest known proto-city. UNESCO World Heritage. Visit the active excavation site under modern protective shelters and the small museum on site. About an hour outside Konya.

06. Konya Bazaar
Bedesten and Aziziye Mosque

The covered Ottoman bazaar between the citadel and the Mevlana Museum. Carpets, copper, prayer beads and the largest bookshop street in central Anatolia. The Aziziye mosque on the bazaar edge has a unique Baroque-Ottoman style.

Food and Cuisine

Konya food is conservative, generous and rooted in central Anatolian wheat country. Etli ekmek, the long flatbread topped with minced meat, is the signature. Slow-cooked tandoor lamb, sac arasi flatbread sandwiches and traditional sweets like sac kavurma round out the menu. Meat is taken seriously, dairy is excellent.

The Signature
Etli Ekmek

A long thin flatbread topped with seasoned minced lamb and fresh herbs, baked in a wood oven. A metre long when served, sliced and shared. The classic Konya lunch. Sifa Restaurant has the most beloved version in town.

Slow Roast
Tandir Kebabi

Whole pieces of lamb cooked slowly in a clay tandoor oven set into the ground. Fork-tender meat served with rice pilav and a slice of bread underneath. The classic Sunday lunch in Konya. Konak Konya Mutfagi has a legendary version.

Hand Pasta
Bamya Corbasi

A clear lamb soup with dried okra. Sour, light and surprisingly elegant. A traditional starter at Konya weddings. Often served with lemon wedges to brighten further.

Sweet Finish
Hosmerim and Kabak Tatlisi

Hosmerim is a fresh cheese dessert with sugar and butter, served warm. Kabak tatlisi is candied pumpkin in syrup served with crushed walnuts and clotted cream. Both classic Konya endings to a heavy lamb meal.

Spiritual Ritual
Sema Ceremony

The Whirling Dervishes ceremony, the meditative spinning that symbolises the soul\u0027s ascent to the divine. Held every Saturday evening at the Mevlana Cultural Centre, free, in modest reverence. The most powerful cultural experience in the city.

Morning Staple
Sac Arasi

Spiced lamb cooked between two iron skillets and served folded into a flatbread sandwich. A late-night Konya favourite, also a classic breakfast. Filling, smoky and addictive.

The pilgrimage address for Konya etli ekmek. Open since 1928, four generations in the same kitchen. Long shared tables, wood ovens, and a long line of locals out the door. Lunch only.

★★★★★ 4.5 (3,200+ reviews)

Slow-cooked tandoor lamb, classic Konya home cooking and one of the best regional dessert menus. A restored stone mansion near the Mevlana Museum. Special-occasion dinner spot.

★★★★★ 4.4 (2,100+ reviews)

A no-nonsense lunch lokanta where local office workers eat. Daily-changing stews, good lentil soup, fresh bread. The cheapest reliable meal in the centre. Lunch only.

Activities and Experiences

01
Mevlana Museum Morning

Go early, before the tour groups arrive. Visit the tomb hall, the dervish lodge, the ceremonial hall and the kitchens. Read the small placards on Sufi practice. Sit in the garden afterwards.

02
Saturday Sema Ceremony

The free Whirling Dervishes ceremony at the Mevlana Cultural Centre, every Saturday evening. Arrive at least 30 minutes early. Silence is requested throughout. Deeply moving even for non-religious visitors.

03
Seljuk Architecture Walk

Alaeddin Mosque, Karatay Medrese and Ince Minare Medrese, plus the Sirceli Mescid in between. Three hours, all within walking distance, the most refined Anatolian Seljuk art on the planet.

04
Catalhoyuk Half Day

An hour south of Konya, the world\u0027s oldest known proto-city. Visit the active excavations under shelters and the on-site museum. Bring a hat and water in summer. Combine with lunch in Cumra on the way back.

05
Bazaar Browse

Konya is famous for prayer beads, religious calligraphy and conservative wedding goods. The Aziziye area bazaars are quieter than Istanbul\u0027s and prices are more honest. Books, beads and tea sets make great Konya souvenirs.

06
Sille Village

A historic Greek-Christian village 12 kilometres from Konya, with a restored Byzantine church and stone-house cafes. A quiet half-day escape with a good lunch stop.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Trabzon

235 km East, about 3 hours
Cappadocia

Konya is the southern gateway to Cappadocia, with regular buses to Goreme and Urgup. Combine your Konya stop with a couple of nights in a cave hotel. The drive itself crosses spectacular Anatolian steppe.

280 km South, about 3 hours
Antalya Coast

A long but beautiful drive south over the Taurus Mountains brings you to Antalya in three hours by car or bus. Easy to pair Konya as a Sufi-and-Seljuk stop on a longer Mediterranean trip.
