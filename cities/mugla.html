The turquoise coast where pine forests tumble into the bluest sea

Places to Visit

01. Bodrum
Bodrum

Ancient Halicarnassus, with the 15th-century Knights' Castle on its harbour. The town still has a small-fishing-village feel in the old streets, with a sophisticated restaurant and beach club scene on the peninsula outside. Yalikavak and Gumusluk are the standout neighbourhoods.

02. Marmaris and Icmeler
Marmaris

A busy holiday resort with a big marina, lively nightlife and quieter coves like Icmeler and Turunc just outside. The starting point for most Blue Cruise sailings. Perfect if you want amenities and easy family beaches.

03. Fethiye and Oludeniz
Fethiye and Oludeniz

Fethiye is the gateway to the Lycian coast. The famous Oludeniz Blue Lagoon, paragliding from Mount Babadag, the Butterfly Valley and the Lycian Way hiking trail all start from here. An outdoor adventure base.

04. Dalyan
Dalyan

A river town with the spectacular rock-cut Lycian tombs of Kaunos above and Iztuzu Beach, a long sandbar that is a protected loggerhead turtle nesting site. Boats chug down the river for a full day of tombs, beach and mud baths.

05. Datca Peninsula
Datca Peninsula

A long thin peninsula stretching west into the Aegean. Quiet villages, small coves, the ruins of Knidos at the very tip. Fewer tourists than Bodrum or Marmaris, a slower pace. Old Datca town is beautiful for an evening walk.

06. Mugla Old Town
Mugla Old Town

The provincial capital sits inland in a valley surrounded by hills. The old town of white Ottoman houses with red-tiled roofs is a UNESCO candidate. Cool in summer, quiet, authentic. Worth a morning if you are driving between coast and Pamukkale.

07. Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon
Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon

The protected lagoon at Ölüdeniz — turquoise water enclosed by a sand spit and forested mountains — is the most photographed natural landscape in Turkey. The Blue Lagoon itself is a national park with entry fee; the adjacent Belcekız beach is free. Paragliding from Babadağ Mountain (1960m) directly above delivers a 45-minute descent over the lagoon. The flight in Turkey with the longest airtime.

08. Köyceğiz Lake
Köyceğiz Lake

A large freshwater lake connected to the sea through the Dalyan Delta, surrounded by reed beds, pine forest and thermal springs. Boat trips from the town of Köyceğiz visit the Dalyan tombs, İztuzu turtle beach and the Sultaniye hot springs on the lake shore. The lake and delta system is protected habitat for loggerhead sea turtles, one of the last intact nesting sites in Turkey.

Food and Cuisine

Mugla's food is Aegean coastal cuisine at its best. Wild herbs foraged from the hillsides, fish and octopus from the sea, olive oil from the slopes around Milas, and vegetables from the fertile inland plains. Lunches are long, mostly outdoors, often right on the water.

The Signature
Grilled Octopus

Octopus tenderised, marinated and grilled over charcoal until the edges are crisp. Served with olive oil, lemon and greens. Every decent meyhane along the coast has it on the menu. The best is at a harbour restaurant with your feet almost in the sea.

From the Sea
Mercan and Sinarit

Sea bream and dentex, the prized white fish of the Aegean. Best simply grilled whole, drizzled with olive oil and served with lemon and sea salt. Pair with a cold glass of local white from one of the Bodrum peninsula wineries.

Aegean Greens
Kopoglu

Fried slices of eggplant and courgette, topped with garlic yogurt and a tomato-butter sauce. A classic summer lunch plate along the coast. Served with warm flatbread to mop up the sauce.

Local Mezze
Tarator

A cold Aegean-style tzatziki made with walnuts, garlic, bread, yogurt and olive oil. Eaten with grilled fish. The walnut gives it a thickness and depth you do not get elsewhere. Every coast meyhane has its own version.

The Ritual
Raki by the Sea

A long dinner of raki, slow mezes, grilled fish and more raki. Started in the late afternoon, finished long after sunset. The Aegean does this better than anywhere else. Not rushed, not casual, this is the meal.

Morning Staple
Aegean Breakfast

Local olives, white cheese, thick olive oil, fresh tomato, sliced cucumber, honey, marmalade, boiled eggs and hot bread. Served on a wooden terrace above the sea. A proper Aegean breakfast takes two hours.

Orfoz

A legendary seafood restaurant on Bitez bay, just outside Bodrum. Tables set on wooden pontoons over the water, served by boat. Raw and cooked seafood menu, bluefin tuna tartare, spiny lobster. One of the best meals in Turkiye.

★★★★★ 4.6 (3,200+ reviews)

Tables on the sand in Gumusluk, facing the sunken Greek city of Myndos across the bay. Perfect grilled seafood, Aegean mezes and a long wine list. Sunset dinners are unforgettable. Book a table for after eight.

Gumusluk seafood
Denizatli Fethiye
★★★★★ 4.6 (2,100+ reviews)

A cozy seafood meyhane in Fethiye's old fish market. Pick your fish from the central hall, pay by weight, then have any of the surrounding restaurants cook it for you. Denizatli is the local favourite. Lively, authentic.

Kitchen Dalyan
★★★★★ 4.8 (1,400+ reviews)

A small chef-owned restaurant in Dalyan, working only with local produce and small-boat fishermen. The menu changes daily. Book a few days ahead. A loving, obsessive kitchen in a quiet town.

A stylish, unfussy seafood restaurant in the Yalikavak marina. Grilled fish, octopus carpaccio, great cocktails and sunset views over the boats. Lunch here, then shop the marina in the afternoon.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Blue Cruise on a Gulet

The classic Mugla experience. Four to seven nights aboard a traditional wooden gulet, sailing from Bodrum, Marmaris or Fethiye. Swim in quiet coves, eat meals on deck, sleep under the stars. Book as a group charter for the best value.

02
Paragliding Oludeniz

Tandem paragliding from the 2000-metre peak of Babadag, landing on the beach at Oludeniz 40 minutes later. Arguably the most scenic paragliding flight in the world. All skill levels welcome with licensed instructors.

03
Lycian Way Hiking

Turkiye's most famous long-distance trail winds for 540 km along the coast through pine forests and ancient ruins. Day sections from Fethiye or Oludeniz are easy to do unguided. Spring and autumn are perfect.

04
Twelve Islands Tour

A full-day boat tour from Fethiye around the twelve small islands just offshore. Swim stops, lunch on board, snorkelling in clear coves. One of the best quick introductions to the coast if you are not doing a full cruise.

05
Knidos Ruins Day

At the very tip of Datca peninsula sit the ruins of the ancient city of Knidos. Two harbours, a theatre, the site of the famous Aphrodite statue. The drive along the peninsula is half the reason to go. Lunch in Datca on the way back.

06
Turtles at Iztuzu

The long sand strip at Iztuzu is a protected nesting ground for endangered loggerhead turtles. Visit the rehabilitation centre or take a boat from Dalyan. Swimming permitted outside nesting hours. A moving visit.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Trabzon

180 km Northeast, about 2 hours
Pamukkale and Hierapolis

The white travertine terraces and the ancient Roman spa city of Hierapolis above. A hot-water thermal pool where you can swim among submerged columns. Long day trip from Bodrum or Fethiye, worth it at least once.

240 km East, about 3 hours
Patara and Kas

Patara has one of the longest empty beaches in the Mediterranean and the ruins of an ancient Lycian capital. Kas, an hour further east, is a charming coastal town with great diving, kayaking over the sunken city of Simena, and a relaxed vibe.
