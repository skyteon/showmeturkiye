Places to Visit in Mugla

A

Bodrum

Bodrum is a beautiful coastal town known for its historic castle, beautiful beaches, and vibrant nightlife. Bodrum Castle (Castle of St. Peter) is a historic fortress dating back to the 15th century, now housing the Museum of Underwater Archaeology with stunning harbor views. The Bodrum Amphitheatre is an ancient Greek theater from the 4th century BC still used for concerts today. Bodrum Marina is a bustling hub of yachts surrounded by shops and restaurants. The Bodrum Market is a vibrant open-air bazaar. Myndos Gate is an ancient gate from the city's fortifications. Bodrum beaches include Gumbet Beach, Bitez Beach, and Ortakent Beach with crystal-clear waters and sandy shores. The Bodrum Windmills are historic windmills that once ground wheat, offering a picturesque backdrop for photos.

B

Marmaris

Marmaris is another popular coastal town that offers beautiful beaches, water sports, and a lively atmosphere. Marmaris Castle is a historic castle dating to the 16th century, now housing the Marmaris Museum with panoramic views. Marmaris Marina is a beautiful marina surrounded by restaurants, bars, and shops, also the starting point for many boat tours and day trips. Marmaris beaches include Cleopatra Beach, Icmeler Beach, and Turunc Beach with crystal-clear waters. Marmaris Grand Bazaar offers traditional Turkish souvenirs, textiles, and jewelry. Marmaris Castle Night Show showcases traditional Turkish music, dance, and culture in a spectacular outdoor setting. Marmaris National Park has scenic hiking trails and stunning views. Atlantis Waterpark features water slides and wave pools. Dalyan Mud Baths offer therapeutic mud and natural hot springs.

C

Fethiye

Fethiye is a picturesque town located on the Turquoise Coast, home to beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and natural wonders like Saklıkent Canyon. Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon is a beautiful natural lagoon with calm and crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming and sunbathing. Saklıkent Gorge is a natural wonder with icy cold waters offering refreshing summer escape. Kayaköy is an abandoned Greek village with empty stone houses, churches, and chapels offering a glimpse into history. Fethiye Castle dates to the 12th century with panoramic views and museum. Butterfly Valley is a nature reserve near Ölüdeniz Beach with waterfall and butterflies. The Lycian Way is a hiking trail stretching over 500 kilometers along the Mediterranean coast with stunning sea views and historic sites. Fethiye Market offers spices, textiles, and souvenirs.

D

Datça

Datça is a beautiful peninsula located in the southwestern corner of Turkey, known for its pristine beaches and unspoiled natural beauty. Knidos is an ancient city on the western tip of Datça Peninsula, a major center of trade and culture in ancient times, now a popular archaeological site with well-preserved ruins. Datça beaches include Palamutbuku Beach, Hayitbuku Beach, and Kargi Bay with clear waters and soft sand. Datça Old Town is a charming area with narrow streets, traditional houses, and variety of restaurants and cafes. Datça Vineyards offer wine tasting and learning about traditional winemaking. Knidos Lighthouse offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding coastline. Datça Nature Park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views.

E

Göcek

Göcek is a beautiful coastal town located on the Gulf of Fethiye, home to stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and a marina popular among yacht owners. Göcek Marina is one of the most popular marinas in the region, offering luxury yacht rentals and a great spot to relax and enjoy beautiful scenery. Dalyan, located near Göcek, is known for stunning natural beauty and ancient ruins. Visitors can take boat tours to see famous Lycian rock tombs and visit mud baths. İztuzu Beach is a beautiful beach near Dalyan, a protected area for endangered loggerhead sea turtles with stunning views. Göcek Islands are small islands perfect for day trips or picnics. Twelve Islands offer stunning beaches, clear waters, and variety of marine life.

F

Köyceğiz

Köyceğiz is a small town located on the shores of Lake Köyceğiz, known for its natural hot springs, mud baths, and beautiful scenery.

Why Visit Mugla?

Muğla stretches across some of Turkey's most photogenic coastline, where pine forests meet turquoise bays and white-washed villages cling to hillsides. Fethiye and Bodrum anchor opposite ends of this region, but the real discovery happens in between: quiet coves you can only reach by boat, fish restaurants where locals still outnumber tourists, and hiking trails that drop you onto beaches with no roads in sight. The water stays warm from May through October, and even in peak summer you can find your own stretch of coast if you're willing to explore.

Food & Cuisine

Muğla Köfte

Muğla Köfte is a popular meatball dish made with ground beef or lamb mixed with spices, breadcrumbs, and eggs. It is usually served with tomato sauce and rice or bulgur.

Deniz Börülcesi

Deniz Börülcesi is a type of seaweed that is popular in Muğla cuisine. It is usually served as a salad with olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic.

Muğla Çöreği

Muğla Çöreği is a sweet pastry made with flour, butter, sugar, and eggs. It is usually served with tea or coffee.

Activities & Experiences

Paragliding

Muğla is a popular destination for paragliding, particularly in the town of Ölüdeniz. You can experience the thrill of flying over the stunning Blue Lagoon and the surrounding mountains.

Scuba Diving

Muğla's clear waters and abundant marine life make it a great destination for scuba diving. You can explore the underwater world and see colorful fish, coral, and even ancient ruins.

Surfing

The Aegean and Mediterranean coasts of Muğla offer great surfing conditions, particularly in the town of Akyaka. You can catch some waves and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the coast.

Rafting

The Dalaman River is a popular spot for rafting in Muğla, with rapids ranging from class II to class IV. You can enjoy a thrilling adventure while taking in the beautiful scenery of the river and its surroundings.

Near Places to Visit

Antalya: Antalya is a city located about 200 kilometers east of Muğla, known for its beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and rich history. Popular destination offering a wide range of cultural and outdoor activities.
