Izmir

The Aegean pearl where ancient history meets relaxed coastal living

Places to Visit

01. Ephesus
Ephesus

One of the best preserved ancient cities in the Mediterranean. The Library of Celsus, the Great Theatre and the marble-paved Curetes Street are all unforgettable. Budget half a day, start early, and add the Terrace Houses ticket to see mosaic-floored Roman villas.

02. Kordon Waterfront
Kordon Waterfront

Izmir's long seafront promenade with palm trees, open-air cafes and ferry terminals. Locals walk, jog and sit here every evening. Catch the sunset ferry across the bay to Karsiyaka and back, one of the most relaxing hours in the city.

03. Kemeralti Bazaar
Kemeralti Bazaar

A sprawling 17th-century bazaar that stretches from the waterfront inland. Spices, ceramics, silver, textiles and some of the best street food in the city. Stop at Kizlaragasi Han, the restored Ottoman caravanserai, for coffee in its courtyard.

04. Alacati
Alacati

A restored Greek stone village turned boutique holiday town. Cobbled lanes lined with bougainvillea, excellent restaurants, small hotels and some of the best windsurfing in the world just outside the centre. Worth two nights on the way through.

05. Cesme Peninsula
Cesme Peninsula

White sand beaches, hot springs, the restored Genoese castle and the laid-back town of Cesme itself. Ilica Beach has shallow warm water ideal for families, while Altinkum to the south is quieter and more beautiful.

06. Agora of Smyrna
Agora of Smyrna

The Roman marketplace of ancient Smyrna, hidden just behind Kemeralti. Vaulted basement galleries, original columns and reliefs. Far less touristed than Ephesus and a reminder that Izmir itself is an ancient city. Less than an hour to visit.

07. Pergamon (Bergama)
Bergama Akropolü

The ancient capital of the Attalid Kingdom and later a major Roman city, 100 km north of İzmir. The Acropolis of Pergamon, on a dramatically steep hill above the modern town of Bergama, holds the foundations of the famous Library, the Temple of Athena and the steepest ancient theatre in the world. The Asklepion healing centre below the acropolis was the Greco-Roman world's most celebrated medical sanctuary.

08. Şirince Village
Şirince

A hillside village 8 km from Selçuk (Ephesus), with old stone and plaster houses, a 19th-century Greek Orthodox church and wine shops selling fruit wines made from local produce. The village was settled by Greek Orthodox Christians until the 1923 population exchange, and the architecture and street layout retain their pre-exchange character. Best visited in the morning before the day-trip crowds from Kuşadası arrive.

Food and Cuisine

Izmir food is pure Aegean. Olive oil, wild herbs, fresh fish and vegetables from fertile hillsides. The city has its own beloved snacks and a strong Greek and Sephardic heritage in the kitchen. Meals here are lighter than the rest of Turkiye, more about quality ingredients than heavy sauces.

The Signature
Kumru

Izmir's famous sandwich. Sesame-crusted bread with sucuk, kasar cheese and tomato, grilled until melted. A Cesme invention that spread across the city. A proper kumru is impossible to eat and walk at the same time.

From the Sea
Cipura and Levrek

Aegean sea bream and sea bass, grilled whole with olive oil and lemon. The fish markets on the Kordon have rows of stalls and simple kitchens out back. Pick your fish, they cook it, and you eat it with salad and white wine by the water.

Aegean Greens
Ot Kavurma

Wild Aegean herbs and greens like sevketi bostan, radika and deniz borulcesi, sauteed with olive oil, lemon and garlic. Served at lokantas and mezehanes. Light, healthy and deeply traditional.

Pastry Icon
Boyoz

A Sephardic flaky pastry brought by Jewish migrants 500 years ago. Plain, or filled with tahini or cheese. Paired with a boiled egg and black tea, this is the classic Izmir breakfast on the street. Altinkapi Boyoz in Alsancak is legendary.

Aegean Ritual
Raki and Mezze

Aniseed spirit poured slowly and iced, then mixed with water until it turns milky white. Drunk alongside a procession of cold mezes and grilled fish in a proper meyhane. Not rushed, not a pre-dinner drink, this is the dinner.

Morning Staple
Gevrek and Boyoz Breakfast

Gevrek is Izmir's name for simit, the sesame-encrusted bread ring. Paired with olives, white cheese, boiled egg and strong tea, it is the no-frills breakfast that Izmir runs on. Best from a street cart on the Kordon.

Where to Eat

Top Restaurants in Izmir

4.5 stars and above, 200 or more reviews on Google

Deniz Restaurant
★★★★★ 4.6 (3,800+ reviews)

An Izmir classic on the waterfront, serving Aegean seafood since 1975. Grilled sea bass, octopus salad, seabream carpaccio. The view of the bay at sunset is the best dinner table in the city.

Aegean seafood, Raki mezze
Asansor Restaurant
★★★★★ 4.5 (2,100+ reviews)

Perched at the top of the historic 1907 elevator in Karatas. Panoramic view over the bay, classic Turkish menu and a Sephardic heritage. A special occasion restaurant steeped in Izmir history.

Turkish classics, Rooftop view
Topcu Restaurant
★★★★★ 4.4 (4,600+ reviews)

Open since 1952, an Alsancak institution for kebabs. Adana, urfa and grilled lamb chops cooked over charcoal. Always busy, always reliable, and completely unpretentious. The classic Izmir dinner.

Charcoal kebabs
Asma Yaprak Alacati
★★★★★ 4.5 (2,800+ reviews)

A stone courtyard restaurant in the heart of Alacati famous for Aegean wild herb mezes. The appetizer trolley rolls to your table with 30 or more small plates. Choose what you want. A magical summer dinner.

Wild herb mezes
Agora Meyhanesi
★★★★★ 4.5 (1,500+ reviews)

A lively Alsancak meyhane with live Turkish music most nights. The full mezze and raki experience, with grilled fish to follow. Book a weekend table at least a few days ahead. Perfect first-night dinner.

Meyhane, Live music
Meshur Sigacik
★★★★★ 4.6 (1,400+ reviews)

A seafood spot in the old harbour of Sigacik, 40 km from Izmir. Whole fish, seafood pasta and sea-view tables on the water. Worth the drive for a long lunch away from the city. Pair with a walk through the old castle village.

Harbour seafood
La Cigale
★★★★★ 4.4 (1,100+ reviews)

A quiet French-Mediterranean restaurant in Alsancak with a short menu that changes monthly. Great wine list, attentive service and a garden for summer dinners. A calm escape from the bustle of the waterfront.

French-Aegean
Dostlar Firin
★★★★★ 4.7 (2,400+ reviews)

The most famous boyoz bakery in Izmir. Hot from the oven, served with a boiled egg and tea. Small, no-frills, and open since 1931. The essential Izmir breakfast stop, especially on a Saturday morning.

Boyoz, Breakfast pastry

Activities and Experiences

01
Ephesus Full Day

A full day at Ephesus, combined with the Virgin Mary's House, the Temple of Artemis and lunch in Sirince village. Drive down in the morning to beat the heat. One of the best single-day archaeology experiences in Turkiye.

02
Kordon Sunset Walk

The ten kilometre promenade from Alsancak to Konak at golden hour. Palm trees, grass lawns, cafes along the water and ferries silhouetted against the sunset. Follow with dinner at one of the open-air fish restaurants by the bay.

03
Windsurfing in Alacati

Alacati's Reef and Altinkum beaches are among the best windsurf spots in the world. Reliable steady winds, shallow water, and plenty of schools offering lessons and rentals. Beginners can learn the basics in a day.

04
Sirince Wine Village

A whitewashed mountain village 40 minutes east of Izmir, famous for fruit wines made from pomegranate, blackberry and quince. Visit in the morning, stop at the small wineries, then have lunch at one of the stone cottage restaurants.

05
Pergamon and Asclepion

The ruins of Pergamon sit on a dramatic acropolis two hours north of Izmir. One of the steepest theatres of the ancient world. Combine with the ancient healing centre of Asclepion at its base. Less visited than Ephesus and just as impressive.

06
Boat Day from Cesme

Take a gulet or speedboat charter from Cesme marina and anchor in the coves of Ildirlik and Karaburun. The water is clear, the hills behind are dry and fragrant with sage. A classic Aegean boat day with swimming, lunch and naps on deck.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Izmir

250 km East, about 3 hours
Pamukkale and Hierapolis

The white travertine terraces of Pamukkale with thermal pools and the ruins of the Roman spa town of Hierapolis above. One of the most photographed sights in Turkiye. An early start makes this workable as a long day trip.

80 km South, about 1 hour
Selcuk and Sirince

Ephesus, the Basilica of St John, the Temple of Artemis and the hilltop village of Sirince in one day. Stay overnight in Sirince for fruit wine and a long slow dinner after the day crowds leave.
