Mediterranean Coast, Turkiye

Antalya

Turquoise waters, ancient ruins and the longest beaches on the Mediterranean

Places to Visit

01. Kaleici Old Town
Kaleici Old Town

The walled historic core of Antalya. Narrow cobbled streets, restored Ottoman mansions, Roman city walls and Hadrian's Gate. The old harbour is ringed with restaurants and bars. The best time to wander is early morning or after sunset when the crowds are thinner.

02. Aspendos Theatre
Aspendos Theatre

Built in 155 AD and the best-preserved Roman theatre in the world. The acoustics still work perfectly. Summer concerts and ballet performances are held here. Combine with a visit to the nearby Roman aqueduct and the ruins of Perge.

03. Kaputas Beach
Kaputas Beach

A small, dramatic cove between Kas and Kalkan with turquoise water and white pebbles. Access is down a long flight of stairs from the coastal road. Arrive early, bring water and a snack. One of the most photographed beaches in Turkiye.

04. Kas
Kas

A small harbour town west of Antalya, beloved for its clear water, sunken Lycian city, diving and boat trips to Kekova Island. Bougainvillea-draped stone houses, small cafes and a laid-back atmosphere. The best base for exploring the Lycian coast.

05. Olympos and Chimaera
Olympos and Chimaera

Ancient ruins scattered through a forest that ends at a beach. Nearby on the mountainside, the Chimaera, eternal flames that burn naturally from the rock. Most atmospheric after dark. Stay in a tree-house hostel in Cirali for the full experience.

06. Duden Waterfalls
Duden Waterfalls

Two waterfalls close to Antalya city. The Lower Duden tumbles 40 metres straight into the sea from a cliff, best viewed from a boat. The Upper Duden has walking paths and a cave you can go behind. A cool break from the summer heat.

07. Düden Waterfalls
Düden Şelalesi

A set of waterfalls on the edge of the Antalya plateau, with the upper falls accessible by park and the lower falls (Aşağı Düden) dropping directly into the sea from a cliff face 20 metres above the Mediterranean. The lower falls are best seen from a boat trip departing from the city harbour. The upper falls park is good for an early morning walk before the site visits.

08. Karaalioglu Park and City Walls
Karaalioglu Parkı

A large public park on the southern edge of Kaleiçi, running along the clifftop above the Mediterranean with the Roman harbour walls visible below. Tall old palm trees and a functioning lighthouse at the far end of the park. The view from the cliff edge - the harbour, the Mediterranean and the Taurus Mountains behind the city - is the standard panoramic image of Antalya.

Food and Cuisine

Antalya's food is pure Mediterranean. Fresh seafood from the harbour, citrus and herbs from the surrounding hills, olive oil from groves that have been worked for 2,000 years. Meals here are long, eaten outdoors, and always better with a view of the water.

The Signature
Piyaz

Antalya's signature cold dish. White beans tossed with tahini, garlic, lemon, vinegar and olive oil, topped with boiled egg and sumac. Light, bright and served everywhere in the region. Best with grilled meat or fish.

From the Sea
Grilled Sea Bass

Levrek, caught the same day and grilled simply with olive oil, salt and lemon. The harbour restaurants in Kas and Kaleici do it best. Served with rocket salad, grilled vegetables and a glass of cold white wine.

Country Kitchen
Sac Kavurma

Lamb cubes cooked on a domed iron pan with peppers, tomatoes and onions. A mountain village dish you find in the roadside restaurants above Antalya. Served with rice and fresh bread, straight from the hot pan to the table.

Sweet Finish
Tahinli Pide

A sweet flatbread filled with tahini and sugar, baked in a wood oven. A traditional Antalya dessert, served warm with a glass of tea. Found at the small bakeries around Kaleici and Muratpasa.

Fresh and Local
Antalya Oranges

The region is famous for its citrus. Fresh orange juice squeezed at roadside stands, orange flower honey and bitter orange jam. At breakfast you will almost always have at least one citrus flavour on the table.

Morning Staple
Kumpir and Gozleme

Giant baked potatoes stuffed with butter, cheese and your pick of fillings, or thin hand-rolled pastry griddled with spinach, cheese or potato. Found at beachside shacks and village stands, perfect after a swim.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Boat Trip to Kekova

A day on the water from Kas or Ucagiz, past the sunken Lycian city visible through the sea floor. Most tours stop at Simena castle, then anchor in coves for swimming and lunch on board. The classic Turquoise Coast experience.

02
Lycian Way Walking

One of the world's great long-distance trails, stretching 540 km along the coast. Most visitors walk a section for a day or two. The stretches between Kas and Kalkan or around Olympos are the most scenic and approachable.

03
Diving and Snorkelling

The water off Kas is some of the clearest in the Mediterranean. Wrecks, reefs, caves and sea turtles. Kas Diving Centre and Bougainville run beginner dives and PADI courses. Snorkelling at Kaputas or Butterfly Valley is just as good.

04
Explore Kaleici After Dark

The old town feels completely different at night. Warm lamplight in the narrow streets, rooftop bars, live music from courtyards, open-air restaurants by the harbour. Start with sunset on the cliff, end with raki at a waterside meyhane.

05
Ancient Ruins Circuit

Aspendos, Perge, Termessos and Side are all within an hour of the city. Two or three of them in a full day makes for one of the best history days in the country. Termessos up in the mountains is the most dramatic and least visited.

06
Paragliding at Babadag

A short drive west to Oludeniz. Tandem flights from the 1,960 metre Babadag mountain float down over the famous Blue Lagoon. No experience needed. One of the top tandem paragliding spots in the world.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Antalya

210 km West, about 3 hours
Oludeniz and Fethiye

The famous Blue Lagoon, Butterfly Valley and the ancient ruins of Tlos. Beach days, paragliding over the lagoon, boat trips to hidden coves. Worth at least one night to do it properly, rather than as a rushed day trip.

65 km East, about 1 hour
Side

A small seaside town built among the ruins of an ancient Greek port. The Temple of Apollo on the headland at sunset is an unforgettable image. Easier and more relaxed than Antalya city, with a long sandy beach.
