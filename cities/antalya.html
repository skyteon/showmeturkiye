Antalya

Overview

Places to Visit in Antalya

A

Düden Waterfall

There are 2 Düden Waterfalls in Antalya because the river goes from the ground and underground. The two rivers converge and disappear into Bıyıklı Sinkhole. The water flows underground for 14 kilometers, emerges from Varsak depression, then comes to surface at Düdenbaşı after two kilometers. The waterfall sits in a small natural park with a cave behind it. You can see it from the seaside by boat or from the cliff observation deck. Beautiful to visit at night with the lights.

B

Perge Ancient City

Perge Ancient City is a very wealthy place in terms of history. There is a theatre, Roman stadium, Roman baths, and long columned streets with Hellenistic touches in the city, which have been preserved until today. It is thought that the acropolis in the city was founded during the Bronze Age. The Archaeological Site of Perge was added to the UNESCO World Heritage Tentative List in 2009.

C

Sandland

Sandland is the most enjoyable open-air museum to visit in Antalya. A tourist destination featuring sand sculptures of world-famous works and mythological creatures. The magnificent works created by professional sand sculpture artists from many countries, using 10,000 tons of river sand in an area of 7,000 square meters, can be visited every day. The museum closes at 11pm. Best to visit at night for better atmosphere with night lights.

D

Karain Cave

Karain Cave is one of the largest natural caves in Turkey. The most beautiful examples of Paleolithic artifacts are the Karain Cave finds. Here, cultural layers belonging to all phases of the Paleolithic Age were unearthed in the filling material, which is approximately 10.5 meters thick.

E

Damlatış Cave

Damlatış Cave was named because of the water drops that continue to drip from the stalactites. In addition to its fascinating beauty, the cave is also famous for its air which is good for asthma patients. Some patients sit in the cave for a certain period under the control of a doctor and apply for a 21-day treatment course. Also, Damlatış Cave is Turkey's first cave opened to tourism.

F

St. Nicholas Church

Santa Claus Church. It is the church in Demre, built to commemorate the death of St. Nicholas, believed to be Santa Claus. It is believed that Santa Claus lay here for a while after his death, and then his bones were taken to Bari by Italian sailors.

G

Lycia Way

The Lycia Way is a walking route created by marking and mapping some of the paths on the Teke peninsula, which were called Lycia in history, starting from Fethiye and extending to Antalya. A long-distance trail in part of the ancient Lycian coast. The marked walking route is 540 km, taking an average of 29 days of walking. The route mainly consists of Roman roads, ancient trails, and mule tracks, often rocky and stony. Between the coast and mountains, the route often has steep elevations.

H

Kurşunlu Waterfall Nature Park

Kurşunlu Waterfall Nature Park is like part of heaven with its wonderful view. The main waterfall is descended from a height of 18 meters. Seven ponds formed with smaller waterfalls formed the Kurşunlu Waterfall Nature Park. Small lakes have a depth of about 6 meters. Entry fee needed.

I

Patara Ancient City

Patara was an ancient and flourishing maritime and commercial city, the capital of Lycia. Patara is a unique place, not only with its history but with its beach and dunes. The Ministry of Culture and Tourism chose Patara Ancient City as the theme of 2020. Patara Beach is the second-longest beach in Turkey at 12km. With its beautiful white sand and dunes in the middle of the beach, it's spectacular. You can also ride a horse for approximately 2.5 hours. Take pictures like you're in the desert!

J

Sapadere Canyon

Sapadere Canyon is 360 meters long. The canyon was formed hundreds of years by the shaping of the rocks by water, wind, and ground movements. There are natural pools and two ponds next to the walking track and recreation facility. The water temperature will be between 0 degrees and 12 degrees in summer. Don't forget your swimsuit! Entry fee needed.

K

Kaputaş Beach

Kaputaş, one of Antalya's most popular beaches with a blue flag, is the closest sandy beach to Kaş, one of the favorite holiday destinations of local and foreign tourists during summer vacation. The crowded beach is reached by descending 187 steps from the highway. Access to the beach is free, but there is a fee to rent sunbeds and umbrellas. The sea is deep blue, slightly wavy, and deepens immediately.

Why Visit Antalya?

Antalya wraps Roman history, mountain peaks, and the Mediterranean into one coastal stretch. You can walk through 2,000-year-old gates in the old quarter, then dive into water so clear it looks filtered. The food here spans fresh seafood pulled straight from the harbor to mountain village cooking brought down from the Taurus peaks. Beaches stay warm enough for swimming well into November, and the old town still feels like a neighborhood instead of a museum.

Food & Cuisine

Taratorlu Piyaz (Haricot Bean Salad with Tarator)

One of the rich tastes of the Mediterranean Region, Piyaz with tarator is the most famous dish of Antalya. In addition to the beans and onions in the classic piyaz, you should try the tarator pide, where boiled eggs and tahini add color and flavor.

Kabak Dessert (Pumpkin with Syrup and Walnuts)

Pumpkin dessert is Antalya's famous dessert, adding flavor to its taste with tahini.

Activities & Experiences

Rafting

Köprülü Canyon Nature Park is the place for rafting in Antalya. The canyon is a valley suitable for rafting starting in Isparta and pouring into the sea in Antalya. There are two historical bridges at the beginning of the rafting area. The small bridge was built by the master, the great bridge was made by the master's journeyman.

Beach Horse Riding

Don't miss the chance of 2 hours of horseback riding pleasure in the woods, countryside, or on the beach in Side when you come to Antalya. Experience riding along the Mediterranean coastline with professional guides.

Near Places to Visit

Isparta: The distance between Antalya and Isparta is 131 kilometers. It takes 1 hour 45 minutes by car. Known for its lavender fields and rose gardens.
