Cappadocia

A lunar landscape of fairy chimneys, cave dwellings and dawn balloons

Places to Visit

01. Goreme Open Air Museum
Goreme Open Air Museum

A UNESCO World Heritage site with a cluster of rock-cut Byzantine churches, chapels and monasteries dating from the 10th to 12th centuries. The frescoes inside the Dark Church are remarkably preserved. Budget two hours and arrive early before the tour buses.

02. Uchisar Castle
Uchisar Castle

The highest point in Cappadocia, a honeycombed rock fortress that was once home to an entire village. Climb the stairs for a 360-degree view over the valleys. At sunset the whole landscape glows orange and pink. The nearby village is the most atmospheric place to stay.

03. Pasabag Valley
Pasabag Valley

Also called Monks Valley, Pasabag has some of the most dramatic fairy chimneys in Cappadocia, many with multiple stone caps. Hermit monks once lived inside them. Easy to reach and walk around. The best spot for classic fairy chimney photos.

04. Rose and Red Valley
Rose and Red Valley

The best hiking valleys in the region. A marked trail runs between Goreme and Cavusin, passing hidden cave churches and pink-orange rock walls. Take two to three hours at sunset when the stone glows. Bring water and good shoes.

05. Derinkuyu Underground City
Derinkuyu Underground City

Eight levels deep, with stables, kitchens, churches, wineries and ventilation shafts. Early Christians carved it out to hide from invading armies. Claustrophobic in parts but completely remarkable. Wear shoes with grip.

06. Love Valley
Love Valley

Famous for its tall, slender rock columns. The best viewing spot is on the ridge between Goreme and Uchisar, a 20-minute walk. At sunrise the balloons drift directly over it. One of the most photographed landscapes in Turkiye.

07. Kaymaklı Underground City
Kaymaklı Yeraltı Şehri

An underground city 20 km south of Nevşehir, second only to Derinkuyu in scale, with eight excavated levels descending 40 metres into the volcanic tufa. Used as a refuge during Byzantine and early Christian periods, with churches, stables, wine cellars and ventilation shafts carved through the rock. Less visited than Derinkuyu and marginally easier to navigate. The two can be combined in a single day.

08. Zelve Open Air Museum
Zelve Açık Hava Müzesi

A valley of abandoned cave dwellings, carved churches and monastic cells north of Göreme, inhabited continuously from the 9th to the 20th century and abandoned only in 1952 when the tufa became structurally unsafe. Three interconnected valleys with hundreds of rooms, chapels and the ruins of a working Byzantine monastery. Less organised than Göreme Open Air Museum but more atmospherically intact.

Food and Cuisine

Cappadocian food is the honest cooking of Central Anatolia. Slow-cooked stews baked in clay pots, hand-rolled mantı, local wines from volcanic soil and small village kitchens that have not changed in generations. Meals in Cappadocia tend to be long, warm and memorable.

The Signature
Testi Kebab

Lamb or beef with vegetables sealed inside a clay jug and slow-cooked over embers for hours. At the table the neck of the jug is dramatically broken with a cleaver. Only found in this region and always worth ordering at least once.

Handmade
Manti

Tiny hand-folded dumplings filled with minced lamb, served with garlic yogurt, melted butter and red pepper oil. Kayseri, just north of Cappadocia, claims to make the best. Properly sized manti are so small that 40 pieces fit on one spoon.

Slow Cooked
Guvec

A stew of meat, vegetables and tomato baked inside a ceramic pot. Every village has its own version. The pot comes straight from the oven to your table, still bubbling. Served with rice and fresh bread.

Local Wine
Cappadocia Wines

One of the oldest wine regions in the world. The volcanic soil gives distinctive character to native grapes like Emir and Kalecik Karasi. Visit the Turasan and Kocabag wineries, or have a glass of local red with dinner in a cave restaurant.

Sweet Finish
Pekmez and Walnuts

Grape molasses drizzled over yogurt or ice cream with toasted walnuts. Also served on bread at breakfast. Cappadocia produces some of the best pekmez in Turkiye because of the local grapes. Small jars make excellent souvenirs.

Morning Staple
Village Breakfast

A spread of local white cheese, olives, honey with honeycomb, tomato, cucumber, eggs, fresh bread and sweet black tea. Best enjoyed on a terrace with the sun rising over the valleys. A full Anatolian breakfast is a small meal on its own.

On the Ground

Activities and Experiences

01
Hot Air Balloon Ride

The classic Cappadocia experience. Take off at dawn and drift over the valleys as the sun rises behind the fairy chimneys. Around one hour in the air. Book two nights in advance in peak season and pray for calm weather.

02
Sunrise at Red Valley

If you cannot get on a balloon, the next best thing is watching the balloons take off from the Red Valley viewpoint. Drive up in the dark and claim your spot. The rocks and balloons glowing pink together at dawn is unforgettable.

03
Valley Hiking

The Rose, Red, Pigeon and Love valleys all have marked walking trails. A half-day loop is easy for most people. Hidden cave churches, pigeon lofts carved into cliffs and quiet picnic spots. Take plenty of water.

04
Stay in a Cave Hotel

Sleeping inside a rock is part of the experience. Museum Hotel, Argos in Cappadocia and Kayakapi Premium Caves are the most special. Even simpler cave guesthouses in Goreme and Uchisar cost far less and are just as atmospheric.

05
Pottery in Avanos

Avanos has been a pottery town since Hittite times, thanks to the red clay from the Kizilirmak river. Workshops let you watch masters throw pots on a foot-powered wheel, and you can try one yourself. Galerie Ikman is worth the visit on its own.

06
Horseback Through Valleys

Cappadocia means "land of beautiful horses" in old Persian. Two or three-hour rides through Rose Valley or up to the Uchisar ridge. Gentle routes for beginners, tougher rides for experienced riders. The views from the saddle are very different.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Cappadocia

80 km North, about 1 hour
Kayseri

The gateway city to Cappadocia and the best place for proper manti. Visit the ancient covered bazaar, the citadel and the Archaeology Museum. A good rainy-day option or a lunch stop on the way out of the region.

80 km West, about 1 hour
Ihlara Valley

A 14-kilometre canyon carved by the Melendiz river, full of Byzantine rock churches and frescoes. A 3-4 hour walk takes you through the best section. Lunch at a riverside restaurant halfway. Cool even in the hottest summer months.
