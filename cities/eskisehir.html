Places to Visit

01. Odunpazari Old Town
Odunpazari

The restored Ottoman neighbourhood, with painted wooden houses, narrow lanes, art galleries and cafes. Mustafa Kemal Pasha\u0027s family home is here. A pedestrianised hilltop district, the heart of photogenic Eskisehir.

02. Odunpazari Modern Museum
OMM Modern Museum

Kengo Kuma\u0027s stacked-wooden-cube building is one of the most beautiful contemporary museums in Turkiye. The collection of modern Turkish art is excellent, and the cafe and gift shop are first class. Allow two hours.

03. Porsuk River Gondolas
Porsuk River

The Porsuk river winds through the centre with hired Venetian gondolas, waterfront cafes and open lawns. Most atmospheric at sunset. Student life on both banks. A truly distinctive Turkish city experience.

04. Kursunlu Complex
Kursunlu Kulliyesi

A 16th-century Ottoman complex designed by Mimar Sinan, including a mosque, madrasah and caravanserai. The inner courtyards now host the Meerschaum Museum and small workshops selling the famous white-stone pipes.

05. Sazova Park
Sazova Science and Culture Park

A huge park with a reconstructed pirate ship, a fairytale castle, a dinosaur park, a planetarium and botanical gardens. Family-friendly, slightly kitsch and beloved by students. A quirky half-day.

06. Eskisehir Bar Street
Hamamyolu

A pedestrianised boulevard with cafes, restaurants, craft stalls and a constant flow of students. One of the most walkable streets in Turkiye and where most evenings begin. Live music at weekends.

07. Atatürk House Museum
Atatürk Evi

A restored early 20th-century house in the Odunpazarı district where Atatürk stayed during the War of Independence, now a museum with period furniture and personal effects. The surrounding streets of the Odunpazarı neighbourhood have the best concentration of traditional bay-windowed timber houses in Eskişehir, with the OMM contemporary art museum nearby for contrast.

08. Hamamyolu Shopping Street
Hamamyolu

The main commercial pedestrian street of central Eskişehir, lined with independent shops, cafes and meerschaum (lületaşı) carving workshops. Eskişehir is the world's primary source of meerschaum, the soft white mineral used to carve pipes and decorative objects. Several workshops on and around Hamamyolu allow visitors to watch carvers at work and purchase pieces directly from the makers.

Food and Cuisine

Eskisehir food has a strong Tatar Turkish heritage thanks to historic migration, which produced cig borek, the flat pastry stuffed with raw minced meat and fried to order. Add the famous balaban kebab, crisp student-priced pide and a big cafe and patisserie culture. Everything is affordable, casual and student-friendly.

The Signature
Cig Borek

A half-moon of dough stuffed with raw minced lamb, onion and spices, fried to order in hot oil. Crisp outside, juicy inside. Eat at Tatar-run shops like Besler Cig Borek, five or six in a sitting.

Local Specialty
Balaban Kebab

A long skewer of cubed lamb cooked over a charcoal pit, served with grilled pepper and tomato on flatbread. Simpler than the better-known urban kebabs but distinctively Eskisehir. Balaban Restaurant is the classic address.

Student Budget
Pide and Lahmacun

Eskisehir students run on cheap wood-fired pide and lahmacun. Minced meat, cheese, spinach, egg, in endless combinations. Two or three pides and an ayran for under 200 lira. An honest, universal lunch.

Sweet Finish
Met Helvasi

A regional sesame and honey halva, much lighter and more fragrant than the supermarket version. Often served at breakfast with kaymak clotted cream. Buy from traditional shops near Odunpazari.

Cafe Culture
Porsuk Coffee Evening

Sit on the grass by the Porsuk river with a specialty coffee or a glass of local wine, watch the gondolas go by and the students circling on bicycles. One of the friendliest evening scenes in the country.

Morning Staple
Patisserie Breakfast

Eskisehir has a serious patisserie culture with dozens of classic and specialty coffee shops. Poppy-seed rolls, cheese borek, fresh-squeezed juice and strong coffee. Start the day at Doga Pastanesi or Cafe Lion.

Activities and Experiences

01
Odunpazari Morning

The painted wooden houses, the Meerschaum Museum, the OMM modern art museum and a lazy coffee at Seyyah. A photogenic half-day that sets the mood for a weekend.

02
Porsuk Gondola Ride

Take a 30-minute gondola ride along the Porsuk river. Yes, real Venetian-style gondolas with Turkish gondoliers. Touristy, fun and unique. Best at sunset.

03
Student Life Evening

Start at Hamamyolu street for early-evening tea. Dinner of cig borek and pide in the student quarter. Finish with beers along the Porsuk. A proper Eskisehir weekend night.

04
OMM Museum

Kengo Kuma\u0027s wooden-cube masterpiece is one of the most beautiful buildings in Turkiye. Allow two hours for the contemporary collection, the cafe and the gift shop.

05
Meerschaum Shopping

Eskisehir is the only significant source of meerschaum, the soft white mineral carved into pipes, figurines and cigarette holders. Visit a workshop in Kursunlu complex, watch the carvers and shop directly.

06
Sazova Park Family Day

The pirate ship, fairytale castle and dinosaur park of Sazova. Kitsch, fun and hugely popular with families. A planetarium and botanical garden too. Half a day with kids.

Extended Itinerary

Day Trips from Eskisehir

155 km North, about 2 hours
Bursa

Pair Eskisehir with Bursa for a long weekend. Early Ottoman monuments, Iskender kebab and Uludag. An easy two-hour drive between the two.

235 km East, about 90 min (train)
Ankara

The fast train makes Ankara a 90-minute trip. Many Ankara residents visit Eskisehir for weekends. Easy to combine, or to stop here between Istanbul and Cappadocia.
