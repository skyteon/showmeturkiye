Balikesir: Top Things to Do and Must-Visit Places for Travelers

June 18, 2023

Planning a trip to Balikesir, Turkiye? Discover the best attractions and activities with our comprehensive guide. From historical landmarks to pristine beaches, this blog post highlights the top places to visit in Balikesir for an unforgettable travel experience.

Mount Ida (Kazdağları)

For the adventure seekers, a trek to the top of Mount Ida, also known as Kazdağları, is a must-do activity in Balikesir. The mountain offers a challenging yet rewarding hike with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. In spring, the slopes are adorned with colorful wildflowers, creating a captivating sight.

One of the most thrilling attractions on Mount Ida is the recently built "Glass Terrace" (Cam Teras). Perched on the peak of Mount Ida, the glass terrace offers a unique and exhilarating experience for visitors. This transparent platform extends out from the cliffside, providing an uninterrupted view of the valleys and mountains below. Walking on the glass floor, you'll feel like you're floating above the stunning landscapes, creating a truly unforgettable and Instagram-worthy moment.

Mount Ida holds significant importance in Greek mythology as the legendary birthplace of Zeus, the king of the gods. According to the ancient tales, it was here that the Trojan prince Paris awarded the golden apple to Aphrodite, leading to the events that triggered the Trojan War.

Ayvalık District

Experience the charm of Ayvalık, a picturesque coastal town known for its cobbled streets, quaint cafes, and stunning sea views. Wander through the town's historic district and admire the beautifully preserved Ottoman architecture. Don't miss a boat trip to the nearby idyllic islands, such as Cunda Island, where you can indulge in delicious seafood and relax on pristine beaches.

Ayvalık is famous for its stunning sunsets over the Aegean Sea. The best places to catch these breathtaking views are from the waterfront promenade or from one of the cozy cafes and restaurants along the coastline. Watching the sun dip below the horizon while casting a warm glow over the sea is an unforgettable experience in Ayvalık.

Aegean Food & Culinary Adventure in Ayvalik

In Ayvalık, prepare to tantalize your taste buds with a mouthwatering array of dishes that celebrate the bountiful flavors of the region.

Olive oil is the star of Ayvalık's culinary scene, and you'll find it infused in almost every dish. Start your day with a traditional Turkish breakfast, featuring freshly baked bread, olives, locally produced cheese, and of course, luscious olive oil drizzled over everything.

Seafood lovers will be in paradise here, as Ayvalık's coastal location ensures an abundance of the freshest catch. Grilled fish, calamari, and octopus dishes are local favorites, bursting with the flavors of the Aegean Sea. Be sure to try "Midye Dolma," stuffed mussels filled with a delightful mix of rice, herbs, and spices, served as a popular street food delicacy.

Cunda Island

Connected to Ayvalık by a causeway, Cunda Island (Alibey Island) is a gem waiting to be explored. This idyllic island exudes a tranquil and bohemian vibe, with its narrow streets, boutique hotels, art galleries, and charming cafes. The island's picturesque waterfront is dotted with fishing boats and sailboats, providing a mesmerizing backdrop for a leisurely evening walk.

Lovers' Hill (Aşıklar Tepesi)

Aşıklar Tepesi is a popular viewpoint located on Cunda Island near Ayvalık. Many visitors come here to witness breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, as the changing colors of the sky create a mesmerizing backdrop for a romantic moment with a loved one.

To reach the top of Aşıklar Tepesi, you can take a short but scenic hike through lush greenery and olive groves. The walk is relatively easy, and along the way, you'll be treated to glimpses of the island's charming beauty. As you near the summit, the panoramic views start to unfold, rewarding you with a magnificent sight of the Aegean Sea stretching out before you.

Once at the top, you'll find a cozy café where you can relax, enjoy a cup of Turkish tea or coffee, and bask in the tranquil ambiance. The hill's elevated position allows you to see the neighboring islands and the picturesque town of Ayvalık on the mainland.

Devil's Table (Şeytan Sofrası)

Devil's Table is a fascinating natural wonder located in Balikesir province. Situated on the slopes of Kaz Mountains, this unique geological formation offers breathtaking views and attracts nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

The Devil's Table gets its name from its distinct flat and table-like appearance. It is essentially a massive limestone plateau with a relatively flat surface, making it an ideal spot for picnics, hiking, and enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

The plateau is perched at an elevation of approximately 1,200 meters (3,937 feet) above sea level, offering visitors stunning vistas of the Aegean Sea, nearby islands, and the lush forests of Kaz Mountains. It's the perfect place to witness spectacular sunrises and sunsets, with the changing colors painting the sky.

Caylak Waterfall & Camping Area

Caylak Waterfall, approximately 5 km from Susurluk district of Balikesir, is located in a lush valley, among the trees with its camping area. The waterfall and the surrounding landscape is suitable for visit purposes, camping and daily picnics.

Caylak Waterfall, where you can cool off on hot summer days and spend a peaceful and pleasant time in nature, stands out with its beautiful views for photography lovers.
