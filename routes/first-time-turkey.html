First Time in Türkiye

Duration7 days Best SeasonApril to May, September to October BudgetMid range PaceBalanced

A first trip to Türkiye should not feel like a checklist. Seven days is enough to walk three places that genuinely change how you think about the country, if you string them in the right order. Istanbul for layered history that you can actually feel under your feet. Cappadocia for landscapes nothing else on earth looks like. Antalya for the Mediterranean coast where Roman theatres sit a kilometre from the sea. You will fly between them, not drive, which keeps the pace human. By the end you will have stood inside a 1500 year old basilica, watched balloons rise over volcanic rock at dawn, and swam under a Roman aqueduct. That is a real first week.

Day 1

Istanbul, the Old City

Land in Istanbul and base yourself in Sultanahmet. This is the historic peninsula, the original Constantinople, where most of what you came to see sits within a fifteen minute walk. Drop your bags, get water, get acclimatised. Jet lag is real and the day is yours to spend lightly.

Start at Hagia Sophia in the late afternoon when the tour groups thin out. Built in 537 under Justinian, it was the largest cathedral in the world for nearly a thousand years before becoming a mosque, then a museum, then a mosque again. The dome still feels improbable from below, and the mosaic fragments along the upper galleries are worth the climb. Allow ninety minutes inside.

Across the square is the Blue Mosque, free to enter outside prayer times. Beneath you, signposted from Hagia Sophia, is the Basilica Cistern, a sixth century underground reservoir that holds water and atmosphere in roughly equal measure. It tends to stay open later than the mosques, which makes it a good last stop before dinner.

Eat in Sultanahmet for ease tonight. Tomorrow goes wide.

Day 2

Istanbul, Bosphorus and Bazaars

Morning at Topkapı Palace. The Ottoman court ran the empire from this hill for nearly four centuries. Buy the Harem ticket separately at the gate, it is worth the extra fee, the rooms are where the family actually lived and the tilework is the strongest in the complex. Three hours minimum, more if you take the audio guide seriously.

Lunch on the slopes down toward Eminönü, then walk through the Spice Bazaar. It is smaller than the Grand Bazaar and a more practical browse, vendors are still real shopkeepers rather than tour funnels. Buy a small bag of Antep pistachios and try a sample of pomegranate syrup.

Afternoon, take the Bosphorus on water. The strait is the actual reason Istanbul exists, and the geography only makes sense when you see it from the middle. The cheapest option is the public ferry from Eminönü to Anadolu Kavağı, two hours each way and almost as good as the tour boats. Sit on the upper deck and watch the city pass.

Dinner across the Golden Horn in Karaköy or Galata. The food is better here than in the old city, and the mood is closer to the Istanbul that locals actually live in.

Day 3

Istanbul to Cappadocia

Morning flight to Nevşehir or Kayseri, around an hour and a half in the air. Both airports sit roughly forty five minutes from Göreme, the village most travellers stay in. Pre book a transfer, taxis at these airports are not always metered.

Sleep in a cave hotel. This is the whole point of being here, and the difference between a regular hotel and a proper kaya otel is genuinely the experience. Most have south facing terraces where you take breakfast looking out at the valleys. Worth choosing carefully.

Afternoon, walk the Göreme Open Air Museum. These are tenth century Byzantine churches carved straight into the volcanic rock, with frescoes still on the walls. The volcanic stone here is called tuff, soft enough to dig with hand tools, hard enough to last a millennium. About two hours of walking.

Sunset at Sunset Point just above town. Reserve a balloon flight for tomorrow at dawn before you sleep. Operators sell out and the day starts at four in the morning.

Day 4

Cappadocia, Balloons and Valleys

Up at four thirty. Pickup at five. You will be in the air at sunrise, watching the valleys change colour as the light hits them, the basket drifting wherever the wind decides. This is the photograph everyone has of Cappadocia and the photograph earns its reputation. About an hour in the air, then a champagne landing at the edge of a field.

Back to the hotel by nine for breakfast. Sleep an hour if you need to.

Late morning, hike one of the valleys on foot. Rose Valley and Red Valley are the most photogenic, named for the colour the rock turns at sunset. Love Valley has the famous fairy chimneys. A two hour walk through any of them is enough to feel the geology, ten million years of volcanic erosion that produced something genuinely unique on earth.

Afternoon back at the hotel. Cave rooms are made for napping. Evening, dinner in Avanos at a pottery house, or quietly in your hotel terrace. Tomorrow is a moving day.

Day 5

Cappadocia to Antalya

Morning, an underground city. Derinkuyu goes deeper, Kaymaklı is wider but easier to walk, both were dug as refuges by early Christian communities and could shelter thousands of people for months at a time. Ventilation shafts, storage rooms, churches, all underground. About ninety minutes inside is enough, longer if you are claustrophobic friendly.

Afternoon flight to Antalya, around an hour. Stay in Kaleiçi, the old town inside the Roman walls. The change in landscape after Cappadocia is the point of the route, you go from volcanic stone to palm trees and Mediterranean light in less than half a day.

Walk the Kaleiçi marina at sunset. The boats lined up below the cliff, the old town behind, the sea opening south. Dinner along the harbour, fish or meze, ideally both.

Day 6

Antalya, Aspendos and Coast

Day trip to Aspendos, a Roman theatre from the second century AD that still stands almost completely intact. The acoustics are good enough that operas are performed there in summer without amplification. About forty five minutes from Antalya by car or organised tour.

If you have time and energy, continue along the coast to Side, an ancient port city with ruins that sit literally on the beach. Otherwise turn back to Antalya and spend the afternoon at Konyaaltı beach. The water is genuinely warm from May to October, and pebble beaches on the western edge of the city give you cleaner water than the sand stretches.

Evening back in Kaleiçi for one more harbour dinner. The clifftop terraces above the marina at Yat Limanı have the longest light.

Day 7

Antalya, Departure

The trip ends with the slowest day on purpose. If your flight is late, walk Kaleiçi one more time, get coffee at a small place in the back streets where the morning light is good, look at the sea. Antalya airport is twenty minutes from the old town and flies direct to most European capitals.

If you want one more thing, the Antalya Archaeological Museum on the western edge of the city is one of the best regional museums in Türkiye, full of Roman statues recovered from the sites you saw yesterday. About an hour, and on the way to the airport.

General Notes

Flights between cities. Book the two domestic legs early. Turkish Airlines and Pegasus both fly Istanbul to Cappadocia, Cappadocia to Antalya. Booked two months out, the total runs around 100 to 180 EUR for both legs combined. Same day bookings can triple that.

Where to stay. Sultanahmet in Istanbul for proximity, Karaköy or Galata if you want a less touristy mood. Cave hotel in Göreme, choose by the terrace view, not the room size. Kaleiçi old town in Antalya, the boutique hotels here have more character than the resorts on the strip.

Season. April through May and September through October are ideal. June through August is hot in Antalya but still good if you accept midday breaks. Avoid winter for this route, Cappadocia gets snow that closes some valleys, Antalya turns rainy and grey.

Visa. Most EU and US passports get a 90 day stamp on arrival. UK passports the same. Check your specific situation a week before flying.

Pace. This is the balanced version. If you want slower, cut Antalya and add days in Istanbul or Cappadocia. If you want more, add Pamukkale between Cappadocia and Antalya for the white travertine pools, costs you a half day of driving but worth it.
