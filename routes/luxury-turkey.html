Luxury Türkiye

Duration8 days Best SeasonMay to June, September to October BudgetPremium PaceSlow

Eight days at premium pace. Three nights in a Bosphorus suite in Istanbul, with private guides for Topkapı and Hagia Sophia. Two nights in a top tier cave spa hotel in Cappadocia, with a private balloon flight rather than a shared one. Three nights on a fully crewed gulet through the Fethiye and Göcek coves, fewer guests, better food, anchor wherever you want. The route is shorter than the standard tours but everything is upgraded. The pace is slow, the comfort is the point, and the experiences are chosen to be the version of each place worth waiting for.

Day 1

Istanbul, Bosphorus Suite

Land in Istanbul, transfer by private car to a Bosphorus suite. The premium hotels along the strait, in Beşiktaş, Ortaköy, or Kuruçeşme, give you water level views from the room and direct access to the waterfront. The suite is the choice over the standard room, the difference is the balcony.

Settle in. The hotel concierge is a real resource at this level, use them for restaurant reservations and any guide requests for the next two days.

Late afternoon, take a private boat on the Bosphorus rather than a tour. Most premium hotels have their own launches or partner with private operators. Two hours on the water at sunset is the right way to see the city's geography.

Dinner at Mikla, Beymen Brasserie, or one of the Bosphorus rooftops with a tasting menu. The Istanbul fine dining scene has expanded significantly in the last ten years, the food matches anywhere in Europe.

Day 2

Istanbul, Old City with Private Guide

Morning with a private guide at Topkapı Palace. Most premium hotels can arrange a licensed guide who knows the building deeply, two to three hours is enough with the right person. The Harem section is a separate ticket and worth the extra fee, the rooms are where the family lived and the tilework is the strongest in the complex.

Lunch at Karaköy Lokantası or one of the upscale meyhanes, mid range pricing for the Istanbul fine dining tier but the food is genuinely good and the experience is local rather than tourist focused.

Afternoon, Hagia Sophia and the Basilica Cistern with the same guide. The basilica side is mosque again, modest dress required, women cover hair in the prayer area. The cistern is fifteen minutes away on foot.

Evening, Hammam at Kılıç Ali Paşa or Çağaloğlu, both historic Ottoman bathhouses restored as functioning hammams. Two hours, the full traditional experience with attendant. Dinner light, the hammam is meant to leave you sleepy.

Day 3

Istanbul, Asian Side and Markets

Morning ferry to Kadıköy on the Asian side with your guide. The market street here is where Istanbul locals shop, no tourists, real fishmongers and butchers and grocers. Try ciğer, the grilled lamb liver from a street stand, then walk to the Moda waterfront for coffee.

Lunch on the Asian side, then ferry back to Beşiktaş. Afternoon at the Çırağan Palace for high tea on the Bosphorus, the restored palace gives you the most genuinely opulent setting in the city for the price.

Late afternoon, optional Grand Bazaar for shopping with your guide who can introduce you to specific dealers for jewellery, antiques, or carpets. The bazaar is overwhelming without context, productive with it.

Last dinner in Istanbul. Tugra at the Çırağan, or one of the Bosphorus rooftops, or a private dinner on the hotel's launch.

Recommended experiences
Çırağan Palace high tea Grand Bazaar with private guide
Day 4

Istanbul to Cappadocia

Morning private flight or business class to Nevşehir. The total transit including ground is around three hours.

Transfer to your premium cave hotel. The top tier in Cappadocia is genuinely impressive, suites with private terraces, hot tubs cut into the volcanic rock, sunken stone bathrooms, breakfast on the terrace. Argos in Cappadocia, Museum Hotel, and a few smaller boutiques are the canonical premium options.

Reserve a private balloon for tomorrow morning, rather than the shared eighteen passenger basket. Private flights with two to four guests run higher per head but the experience is meaningfully different, smaller basket means more elbow room and a different flight pattern.

Settle in. Late afternoon, walk to Sunset Point just above the village. Dinner at the hotel restaurant, several have proper tasting menus with regional Anatolian cuisine.

Day 5

Cappadocia, Private Balloon and Spa

Up at four thirty for the private balloon. Smaller basket, less wait at the launch site, more flight time at higher altitude. About 75 minutes in the air, then a champagne landing in a field with a small ceremony.

Back to the hotel by nine for breakfast on your terrace. The morning is for sleeping or for the spa.

Cave spa at the hotel, most premium kaya hotels have full spas built into the volcanic rock, with hammam, indoor pool, and treatment rooms. Two to three hours, the contrast with the outdoor light from the terrace is part of the experience.

Late afternoon, private guided walk through one of the valleys. Rose Valley or Red Valley, the same routes the public tours take but at your pace and with the guide focusing on what you find interesting. About two hours.

Evening, dinner at the hotel or at one of the cave restaurants. Lil'a at the Museum Hotel and Topdeck in Göreme are the established fine dining options in the region.

Cave spa treatment Private valley guided hike
Day 6

Cappadocia to Muğla, Onto the Boat

Morning private transfer to Nevşehir airport, fly to Dalaman via Istanbul, around four hours total with the connection.

Private transfer from Dalaman to the marina at Göcek or Fethiye, around forty minutes. Board your private gulet. At this tier, the boat is a fully crewed wooden gulet for two to four guests rather than the eight to twelve passenger boats. Larger cabins, real bathrooms with showers, a captain who knows the coves and a chef who handles all meals.

Sail in the afternoon. Anchor for the first night in Göcek harbour or one of the nearby coves. Dinner on board, the chef will have shopped in the morning and produces a meal that compares to coastal restaurants ashore.

Sleep on the boat. The cabins on premium gulets are properly air conditioned and the windows give you the water at any hour.

Day 7

Onboard, Day Two

Slow morning. Breakfast on deck, swim in clear water before the sun is high.

Cruise west through the Twelve Islands. The captain will take you to the coves you cannot reach by car, including Tersane Island with its half submerged Byzantine ruins, Cleopatra Beach on Sedir Island, and several smaller anchorages where you are likely the only boat.

Lunch at anchor in a quiet bay, fresh fish from the morning, salad with herbs from the harbour market, fruit from local orchards. The wine selection on premium gulets is properly stocked.

Afternoon, optional water sports, the boat carries paddleboards, snorkelling gear, and a tender for shore visits. Or simply read on deck.

Evening, anchor for the second night in another quiet cove. Dinner under stars.

Twelve Islands gulet cruise Private cove swimming
Day 8

Onboard Day Three, Departure

Last morning on the boat. Breakfast on deck, final swim. The boat returns to the marina by mid afternoon.

Disembark, private transfer to Dalaman airport, around forty minutes. Direct flights to most European capitals.

If your flight is late, the boat can extend an hour in the morning for one more cove visit, or you can spend an hour at the hotel pool back in Göcek. The route ends at the slowest possible pace, which is the right shape for it.

General Notes

Flights and transfers. Premium routing uses business class for the long haul and either business class domestic or private charter for the Istanbul to Nevşehir leg. Domestic business runs around 250 EUR each way, private charter for two to four guests around 4000 EUR per leg. Private transfers between airports and hotels are standard at this tier.

Where to stay. Bosphorus suite hotels include Çırağan Palace Kempinski, Four Seasons Bosphorus, Mandarin Oriental Bosphorus, Six Senses Kocataş Mansions. Cappadocia premium options include Argos in Cappadocia, Museum Hotel, Sacred House. Most are family run boutique rather than chain hotels.

Boats. Private crewed gulets at this tier run roughly 4000 to 8000 EUR per night for two to four guests, including all meals, drinks, and crew. Two to three night charters from Göcek or Fethiye are the standard route. Book six months ahead for July to August, three months for shoulder season.

Season. May to June, September to early October. The premium hotels are open year round but the boat season runs early May to late October. Peak prices and crowds in July and August, shoulder months are calmer and the light is better.

Pace. Eight days at this pace is built for slow travel, with one day to settle into each place and one day to actively use it. Cutting to six days drops one Istanbul day and one boat day, which works for travellers with less time. Adding more time, the natural extension is one more day in Cappadocia or two more on the boat, both worth the extra cost.
